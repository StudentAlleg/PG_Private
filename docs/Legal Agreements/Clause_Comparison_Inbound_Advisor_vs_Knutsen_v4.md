# Clause-by-Clause Comparison
## Inbound Advisor NDA (Template) vs. Knutsen v4 (NDA + IP Assignment)

**Date:** March 17, 2026
**Prepared by:** Mike Leavenworth (with AI assistance)

**Document A:** `Boilerplate_Mutual_NDA_Inbound_Advisor Project Green.md` — Generic template for any advisor joining Project Green's advisory board. Confidentiality only.

**Document B:** `MutualNDA_IP_ProjectGreen_Knutsen_v4_Leavenworth_Amendments.md` — Kris Knutsen-specific agreement. NDA + IP Assignment with three exhibits.

---

## Executive Summary

These documents share a common DNA but serve fundamentally different purposes:

| | Inbound Advisor NDA | Knutsen v4 |
|---|---|---|
| **Purpose** | Confidentiality only | Confidentiality + IP Assignment |
| **Scope** | Template for any advisor | Named agreement for Kris Knutsen |
| **IP treatment** | Explicitly no IP assignment (Section 2.3) | Full IP assignment framework (Section 3) with Pre-Existing IP schedules and work-for-hire |
| **Governing law** | Florida | Delaware |
| **Term** | 2 years, 30-day termination | No fixed term — tied to board relationship |
| **Exhibits** | None | A (Knutsen Pre-Existing IP), B (Company Pre-Existing IP), C (Work-for-Hire Schedule) |
| **Sections** | 12 sections | 8 sections (but denser, with sub-sections) |

The Inbound Advisor NDA is a lighter-weight document designed for advisors who provide guidance but don't create deliverables. The Knutsen v4 is a comprehensive agreement for an advisor who both advises and performs contracted work that produces Company-owned IP.

### Intellectual Property — The Biggest Structural Difference

The Inbound Advisor NDA and Knutsen v4 take opposite approaches to IP:

#### Inbound Advisor: Section 2 — IP Acknowledgments (Confidentiality-Only)

| Clause | Content | Knutsen v4 Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 2.1 Company IP | Company retains all its IP | 3.3 Company IP Ownership (same) |
| 2.2 Advisor's Existing Ventures | Company has no rights in Advisor's ventures | Not explicitly stated — covered by Pre-Existing IP (3.1) and Exhibits |
| **2.3 No IP Assignment** | **"This Agreement does not assign, license, or transfer any IP"** | **No equivalent — Knutsen v4 DOES assign IP (Section 3.2)** |
| 2.4 Feedback | Advisor assigns Feedback to Company (patents, copyrights) | Not in Knutsen v4 — replaced by Advisor Work Product (3.9) |
| **2.5 No Inevitable Disclosure** | **Parties agree inevitable disclosure doctrine doesn't apply** | **Not in Knutsen v4** |
| 2.6 Advisor Work Product | Advisor owns advisory work product | 3.9 Advisor Work Product (same substance) |

#### Knutsen v4: Section 3 — Intellectual Property (Full Assignment Framework)

| Clause | Content | Inbound Advisor Equivalent |
|---|---|---|
| 3.1 Pre-Existing IP | Listed in Exhibits A/B, remains with owner. License granted if incorporated into Company work product. Material Breach definition. Conceptual derivatives provision. | 2.2 (partial — acknowledges existing ventures but no license framework) |
| **3.2 Assignment of Contributions** | **IP created at Company direction under Exhibit C is irrevocably assigned to Company. Work-for-hire. Personal time carve-out with 3 criteria.** | **No equivalent — Inbound explicitly prohibits this (Section 2.3)** |
| 3.3 Company IP Ownership | Same as Inbound 2.1 | 2.1 Company IP |
| **3.4 Further Assurances** | **Knutsen must sign IP documents. Power of attorney if he doesn't. Deliverable acceptance mechanism.** | **No equivalent** |
| **3.5 No License to Knutsen** | **Nothing grants Knutsen rights to Company IP** | **No equivalent (not needed — no IP flows in Inbound)** |
| **3.6 Future Licensing** | **Acknowledges potential for separate licensing deals** | **No equivalent** |
| **3.7 Federal IP Protections** | **DTSA exemplary damages for trade secret misappropriation** | **7.2 Damages (similar but in Remedies section)** |
| **3.8 Compensation for Additional IP** | **Company must negotiate compensation for IP beyond Exhibit C** | **No equivalent — no IP contributions expected in Inbound** |
| 3.9 Advisor Work Product | Knutsen owns advisory output | 2.6 Advisor Work Product (same substance) |

**Key differences in detail:**

1. **Feedback Clause:** The Inbound Advisor NDA (Section 2.4) assigns all feedback (ideas, suggestions, recommendations) that constitutes patentable or copyrightable subject matter to the Company. Knutsen v4 has NO feedback clause — instead, it has Advisor Work Product (Section 3.9) which says advisory output is owned by Knutsen. This is a deliberate design difference: the Inbound template gives the Company rights to informal feedback; the Knutsen agreement gives Kris ownership of his advisory output (with IP assignment limited to Exhibit C deliverables only).

2. **No Inevitable Disclosure:** The Inbound Advisor NDA (Section 2.5) explicitly rejects the inevitable disclosure doctrine. Knutsen v4 does not address this. The Inbound template includes it because advisors with no IP obligations might be accused of inevitably disclosing CI through their other work. The Knutsen agreement doesn't need it because IP assignment provides stronger protections.

**Key differences:**

### Term & Termination

| Feature | Inbound Advisor | Knutsen v4 |
|---|---|---|
| **Fixed term** | **2 years from Effective Date** (Section 6.1) | **No fixed term — tied to board relationship** |
| **Termination** | **30 days' written notice by either party** (Section 6.2) | **No explicit termination provision (beyond Change of Control)** |
| CI survival | 5 years from disclosure or 5 years from termination, whichever is later (Section 6.3(a)) | 5 years from disclosure or 5 years from termination, whichever is later (Section 2.6) |
| Trade secret survival | Perpetual while qualifying; falls back to 5-year if ceases to qualify (Section 6.3(b)) | Identical (Section 2.6) |
| Return/destruction | **15 business days** (Section 6.4) | **10 business days** (Section 2.7) |
| Both include digital reality provisions | Backup systems, archival copy for counsel | Identical substance |
| **Change of Control** | **Mutual — either party** (Section 6.5). 10-day notice, 15-day termination right. | **Company-only trigger** (Section 8.11). Same timelines. Adds: "no new CI after termination." |

**Key differences:**

1. **Fixed term:** The Inbound NDA automatically expires after 2 years unless renewed. The Knutsen agreement runs indefinitely while Kris serves as board member. This makes sense — the Knutsen agreement governs ongoing IP contributions that shouldn't expire on a clock.

2. **Termination provision:** The Inbound NDA allows either party to terminate with 30 days' notice, no reason needed. Knutsen v4 has no general termination clause — the relationship ends when the board membership ends. This is a notable gap in Knutsen v4.

3. **Return/destruction timing:** Inbound allows 15 business days; Knutsen requires 10 business days. The tighter Knutsen timeline may reflect the higher sensitivity of IP-related materials.

4. **Change of Control scope:** Inbound applies to either party's change of control; Knutsen applies only to the Company's change of control. This makes sense — Kris is an individual, not an entity that could undergo a change of control.

## Observations for Future Drafting

1. **Non-Solicitation gap in Knutsen v4:** The Inbound NDA includes a 12-month mutual non-solicitation clause. Knutsen v4 does not. Consider whether this was intentional or should be added.

2. **Referencing the Relationship gap in Knutsen v4:** The Inbound NDA governs public references (LinkedIn, etc.). Knutsen v4 has no equivalent. Consider adding if Kris's public association with Project Green matters.

3. **Termination gap in Knutsen v4:** The Inbound NDA has a clean 30-day termination provision. Knutsen v4 has no general termination clause beyond Change of Control. The agreement effectively runs until the board relationship ends, but "ends" is not defined.

4. **Feedback vs. Advisor Work Product:** The Inbound NDA assigns informal feedback to the Company (Section 2.4). Knutsen v4 gives Kris ownership of advisory output (Section 3.9). These are opposite approaches. When adapting the Inbound template for a new advisor, decide which approach is appropriate based on whether the advisor will also have an IP assignment agreement.

5. **Governing law alignment:** The Inbound template uses Florida law; Knutsen uses Delaware law. Once the Delaware C-Corp is formed, consider whether the Inbound template should switch to Delaware law for consistency.

6. **Exclusion (b) strictness:** The Inbound NDA requires "written records" to prove prior knowledge; Knutsen v4 requires only "without restriction." The Inbound standard is harder to meet but protects both parties better.
