# MUTUAL NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of [DATE] ("Effective Date") by and between:

**[COMPANY NAME]**, a [STATE OF INCORPORATION] [ENTITY TYPE] with its principal place of business at [ADDRESS] ("Company"); and

**Michael Leavenworth**, an individual residing in the State of Florida ("Advisor").

Company and Advisor are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## Recitals

The Parties wish to explore or continue an advisory relationship in which the Advisor serves on the Company's advisory board. The Parties acknowledge that the Advisor's role on the Company's advisory board is advisory and non-fiduciary in nature. The Advisor does not owe fiduciary duties to the Company by virtue of this advisory relationship. Accordingly, the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement constitute the primary contractual framework governing the protection of Confidential Information disclosed to the Advisor, and both Parties agree to strictly comply with such obligations.

In connection with this relationship, each Party may disclose confidential and proprietary information to the other. This Agreement establishes the terms under which such information will be shared and protected.

---

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, in any form or medium (oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Business plans, financial projections, revenue data, and pricing strategies;
- Software, source code, algorithms, system architectures, and technical documentation;
- Product roadmaps, feature specifications, and research;
- Customer lists, prospect data, and partnership agreements;
- Personnel information and compensation structures;
- Personal information and financial information of either Party disclosed in connection with the advisory relationship, regardless of whether such information relates to the Party's business operations;
- Trade secrets as defined under the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act (Fla. Stat. §688.002(4)) and the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1839).

The Parties agree that information shared in advisory board meetings, whether oral, written, or electronic, shall be presumed confidential unless expressly designated as non-confidential by the Disclosing Party.

### 1.2 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as demonstrated by written records;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information;

(d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority (subject to Section 3.3 below).

The burden of proof to establish any exclusion rests with the Receiving Party. The fact that individual elements of Confidential Information are publicly available does not make a combination or compilation of such elements public.

### 1.3 Trade Secrets

"Trade Secrets" means any Confidential Information that: (i) derives independent economic value from not being generally known or readily ascertainable; and (ii) is subject to reasonable measures to maintain its secrecy, as defined under the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act (Fla. Stat. §688.002(4)) and the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1839). For purposes of this Agreement, information shall only be treated as a Trade Secret if it meets the statutory requirements at the time enforcement is sought.

### 1.4 Residual Knowledge

"Residual Knowledge" means general ideas, concepts, know-how, methodologies, and techniques that are retained in the unaided memory of a person who has had access to Confidential Information, provided that such person has not intentionally memorized specific Confidential Information for the purpose of retaining and subsequently using or disclosing it. Neither Party's confidentiality obligations under this Agreement restrict the use of Residual Knowledge; however, this Section does not grant a license under any patent, copyright, or other intellectual property right, nor does it permit the disclosure of specific, identifiable Confidential Information such as financial figures, customer names, pricing terms, source code, or proprietary formulas.

### 1.5 General Strategic Advice

For the avoidance of doubt, Confidential Information does not include general strategic advice, recommendations, or observations provided by either Party that reflect that Party's cumulative professional experience and judgment, even where such advice may be informed by patterns observed across multiple advisory engagements, provided that such advice does not reveal specific, identifiable Confidential Information of either Party.

## 2. Advisor's Existing Ventures and Work Product

### 2.1 Acknowledgment

The Company acknowledges that the Advisor is the founder and owner of separate business ventures and intellectual property that exist independently of this advisory relationship. The Advisor's participation on the Company's advisory board does not grant the Company any rights, license, or interest in the Advisor's separate ventures, intellectual property, or business activities, whether or not they operate in related or adjacent fields.

### 2.2 No Implied License or Assignment

Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as an assignment, license, or transfer of any intellectual property owned by the Advisor, whether created before, during, or after the term of this Agreement. Any use of the Advisor's intellectual property by the Company requires a separate written agreement signed by both Parties.

### 2.3 Other Advisory Relationships

The Company acknowledges that the Advisor may serve on advisory boards or provide consulting services to other companies, including companies that may operate in related or overlapping fields. This Agreement does not restrict the Advisor from engaging in such relationships, provided the Advisor complies with the confidentiality obligations herein.

### 2.4 No Inevitable Disclosure

The Parties agree that the "inevitable disclosure" doctrine shall not apply to this Agreement. The Advisor's service on other advisory boards, including those of the Company's competitors, shall not constitute a breach of this Agreement absent proof of actual disclosure or use of specific Confidential Information. The Parties acknowledge that Florida law does not recognize the inevitable disclosure doctrine, and neither Party shall invoke it in connection with this Agreement.

### 2.5 Advisor Work Product

Any advice, recommendations, analysis, strategies, or other work product provided by the Advisor in connection with the advisory engagement ("Advisor Work Product") shall be owned by the Advisor unless explicitly assigned to the Company in a separate written agreement. For the avoidance of doubt, Advisor Work Product is not Confidential Information of the Company, and the Advisor may provide similar or identical advice, recommendations, or strategies to other parties, provided that the Advisor does not disclose specific Confidential Information of the Company in doing so.

## 3. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

### 3.1 Non-Disclosure

Each Party, when acting as the Receiving Party, agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence using no less than reasonable care, and no less care than it uses to protect its own confidential information of like kind;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of providing or receiving advisory services as contemplated by this Agreement or any separate advisory agreement between the Parties;

(d) Not use Confidential Information for personal gain, competitive advantage, or any purpose outside the scope of the advisory relationship;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or extract Confidential Information beyond what is reasonably necessary for the stated purpose;

(f) Not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, structure, or design of any Confidential Information, including software, algorithms, or technical systems.

### 3.2 Permitted Disclosures

Either Party may disclose the other Party's Confidential Information to:

(a) Legal counsel, accountants, or financial advisors retained by the Receiving Party who are under a professional or contractual duty of confidentiality, and only to the extent necessary for legal, accounting, or financial advice;

(b) Employees and contractors of the Receiving Party who have a legitimate need to know in furtherance of the advisory relationship and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;

(c) Government or regulatory bodies as required by law — subject to the notice obligations in Section 3.3.

For the avoidance of doubt, "permitted recipients" under clause (b) does not include investors, portfolio company personnel, or other advisory board members unless the Disclosing Party provides prior written consent. The Receiving Party shall identify any permitted recipient to the Disclosing Party upon reasonable written request and shall remain responsible for any breach of this Agreement by such recipient.

### 3.3 Legal Compulsion

If either Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information of the other Party, the compelled Party shall: (i) provide prompt prior written notice to the other Party (to the extent legally permissible) to enable that Party to seek a protective order or other remedy; (ii) cooperate reasonably with such efforts; and (iii) disclose only that portion of Confidential Information legally required.

### 3.4 Breach Notification

Each Party shall notify the other Party promptly — and in no event later than five (5) business days — upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, loss, or misuse of the other Party's Confidential Information. Such notice shall describe in reasonable detail: (i) the circumstances of the incident, (ii) the categories of Confidential Information affected, and (iii) the steps being taken to mitigate harm. The notifying Party shall cooperate reasonably with the other Party's investigation.

### 3.5 Scope of Disclosure

The Company shall limit the Advisor's access to Confidential Information to that which is reasonably necessary for the Advisor's advisory role. The Advisor shall not be deemed to have received Confidential Information solely by virtue of having access to general communication channels, repositories, or collaboration platforms (such as Slack, Discord, or Teams) unless the Advisor actually accesses specific Confidential Information therein that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential. The Company shall not provide the Advisor with access to systems, channels, or repositories beyond those reasonably necessary for the advisory engagement without the Advisor's prior written consent.

### 3.6 Information Barriers

The Advisor acknowledges that serving on multiple advisory boards creates a risk of inadvertent cross-pollination of Confidential Information. The Advisor agrees to maintain reasonable information barriers to prevent the Company's Confidential Information from being disclosed to, or used for the benefit of, any other entity for which the Advisor provides advisory services. The Advisor shall not disclose or use the Company's specific Confidential Information in connection with any other advisory engagement.

### 3.7 Oral Disclosures

Confidential Information disclosed orally or visually shall be identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing by the Disclosing Party within ten (10) business days. Failure by the Disclosing Party to provide a written summary within this period does not waive protection if the information would be clearly recognized as confidential by a reasonable person familiar with the advisory context. The following categories of information shall always be deemed Confidential Information regardless of marking or written follow-up: (a) financial statements and projections, (b) customer and client lists, (c) pricing and compensation structures, (d) product roadmaps and unreleased features, and (e) personnel information.

### 3.8 Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

Neither Party shall input, upload, or otherwise process the other Party's Confidential Information using any publicly available or consumer-grade generative artificial intelligence, large language model, or similar machine learning system, unless: (i) the system is deployed within the Receiving Party's own controlled infrastructure; (ii) the system does not use Confidential Information for model training or improvement; and (iii) the Disclosing Party has given prior written consent. For the avoidance of doubt, derived artifacts — including embeddings, vector representations, model weights, summaries, and log files — generated from Confidential Information are themselves Confidential Information and subject to all obligations under this Agreement.

## 4. Term and Termination

### 4.1 Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for two (2) years from the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier by either Party or extended by mutual written agreement.

### 4.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

### 4.3 Survival of Obligations

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement:

(a) **General Confidential Information:** Confidentiality obligations shall survive for five (5) years from the date of disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information, or five (5) years from termination of this Agreement, whichever is later.

(b) **Trade Secrets:** Confidentiality obligations shall remain in effect for so long as the information qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law, with no fixed expiration. If the information ceases to qualify as a Trade Secret under Fla. Stat. §688.002(4), the five-year survival period in clause (a) shall apply from the date the information ceased to qualify.

### 4.4 Return or Destruction

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall within fifteen (15) business days: (i) return all tangible Confidential Information; (ii) permanently delete all electronic copies within the Receiving Party's reasonable control, including from local devices, cloud storage, and collaboration platforms; and (iii) certify such return and deletion in writing upon request. The Receiving Party acknowledges that copies may exist in automated backup systems not within its reasonable control; provided that: (a) the Receiving Party shall not intentionally access or restore such copies for any purpose other than disaster recovery, and (b) any copies retained in automated backup systems remain subject to all confidentiality obligations under this Agreement. One archival copy may be retained by counsel solely for legal compliance purposes, and such retained copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations.

### 4.5 Change of Control

In the event of a Change of Control of either Party — defined as any merger, acquisition, sale of substantially all assets, or transfer of more than fifty percent (50%) of voting equity to a third party — the Party undergoing the Change of Control shall notify the other Party in writing within ten (10) business days of closing. The other Party may terminate this Agreement upon fifteen (15) days' written notice following receipt of such notification.

## 5. Scope and Limitations

### 5.1 Confidentiality Only

This Agreement addresses confidentiality only. It does not establish:

(a) Any obligation to enter into a business relationship, transaction, or investment;

(b) Any obligation by the Advisor to assign intellectual property to the Company;

(c) Any compensation, equity, or other consideration for the Advisor's participation;

(d) Any employment, partnership, joint venture, or agency relationship;

(e) Any exclusivity or non-compete obligation on either Party.

Any such arrangements require a separate written agreement signed by both Parties.

### 5.2 Conflicts of Interest

The Advisor shall promptly disclose to the Company any material conflict of interest that may arise from the advisory relationship. The Advisor's service on other advisory boards or involvement in other ventures does not, by itself, constitute a conflict of interest.

### 5.3 Non-Solicitation

During the term of this Agreement and for twelve (12) months following termination, neither Party shall directly solicit for employment or engagement any employee or contractor of the other Party with whom the soliciting Party had direct interaction during the advisory relationship. This restriction does not apply to: (i) general public advertisements or job postings not specifically targeted at the other Party's personnel, or (ii) any individual who initiates contact with the soliciting Party without prior solicitation.

### 5.4 No Warranty

Neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or fitness for a particular purpose of any Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement. Each Party acknowledges that it shall rely solely on its own judgment and assessment regarding such information.

## 6. Privacy

### 6.1 Personal Data

Each Party agrees to handle any personal data of the other Party or of the other Party's customers, employees, or contractors in compliance with applicable privacy laws, including but not limited to applicable state privacy statutes and federal law.

### 6.2 No Unauthorized Collection

Neither Party shall collect, store, or process personal data obtained through the advisory relationship for any purpose other than fulfilling the advisory engagement. To the extent Confidential Information includes personal data as defined under applicable privacy laws, such data shall be handled in compliance with all applicable data protection and privacy laws, which shall take precedence over any conflicting provisions of this Agreement.

## 7. Remedies

### 7.1 Injunctive Relief

Each Party acknowledges that any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the non-breaching Party is entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the requirement to post bond or prove actual damages, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity. The Parties acknowledge that a court of competent jurisdiction retains full discretion over the granting of equitable relief.

### 7.2 Damages

For the avoidance of doubt, no limitation on damages — including indirect, special, incidental, or consequential damages — shall apply to breaches of the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement. In cases of willful or malicious misappropriation of Trade Secrets, the non-breaching Party may seek exemplary damages of up to two times (2x) actual damages and attorney's fees as provided under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1836(b)(3)) and the Florida Uniform Trade Secrets Act (Fla. Stat. §688.004).

### 7.3 Attorney's Fees and Costs

The prevailing Party in any action, suit, or proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorney's fees, court costs, and all other litigation expenses, including but not limited to expert witness fees, e-discovery costs, and fees incurred in establishing entitlement to or the amount of fees and costs.

## 8. Dispute Resolution

### 8.1 Mediation

The Parties agree to attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under or relating to this Agreement through mediation before initiating litigation. Mediation shall be conducted in accordance with Chapter 44, Florida Statutes, by a mutually agreed-upon mediator. If the Parties cannot agree on a mediator within fifteen (15) days of a written request for mediation, either Party may proceed to litigation under Section 8.2.

### 8.2 Governing Law and Exclusive Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Florida, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Florida. Each Party irrevocably submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of such courts, waives any objection to venue or jurisdiction, and waives any claim of forum non conveniens. Any action filed in violation of this Section may be transferred or dismissed on the basis of this clause.

### 8.3 Waiver of Jury Trial

EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES THE RIGHT TO TRIAL BY JURY IN ANY ACTION, SUIT, OR PROCEEDING ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT.

## 9. Whistleblower and Government Reporting

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, nothing herein shall prohibit either Party from: (i) reporting possible violations of law or regulation to any governmental authority, including the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission, the Department of Justice, or any state agency; (ii) making disclosures protected under whistleblower provisions of applicable federal or state law, including the Florida Private Sector Whistleblower Act (Fla. Stat. §448.102) and the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (15 U.S.C. §78u-6); or (iii) filing a charge with or participating in any investigation or proceeding conducted by any governmental authority. Neither Party is required to notify the other prior to making any such report or disclosure.

## 10. DTSA Immunity Notice

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1833(b)), each Party is hereby notified that:

(a) An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

(b) An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual (i) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (ii) does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.

**11.7 Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party. In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all of a Party's assets, the provisions of Section 4.5 (Change of Control) shall apply.

**11.8 Good Faith.** The Parties agree to act in good faith toward one another in all matters arising under or related to this Agreement, including the interpretation of ambiguous provisions and the resolution of unforeseen circumstances.
