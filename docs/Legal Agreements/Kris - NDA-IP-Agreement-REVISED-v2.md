# Mutual Non-Disclosure & IP Assignment Agreement

**Project Green (AI Digital Twin for Accounting Firms)**

---

## Preamble

This Mutual Non-Disclosure and IP Assignment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 6, 2026 ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Kristopher Knutsen** ("Knutsen"), an individual residing in the State of Colorado, serving as Board Member of Project Green; and

**Michael Leavenworth** ("Leavenworth"), Founder of Project Green, an individual residing in the State of Florida, acting on behalf of Project Green, a Delaware C-Corporation in formation ("Company").

Knutsen and Leavenworth are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

Each Party anticipates disclosing Confidential Information to the other in connection with Project Green. In any given disclosure, the Party disclosing information is the "Disclosing Party" and the Party receiving information is the "Receiving Party." Both Parties are simultaneously bound by the obligations of both roles.

In consideration of (i) the mutual exchange of Confidential Information, (ii) Knutsen's continued appointment as Board Member under the Terms Memo, (iii) access to each other's Confidential Information including source code, business plans, technical documentation, and cybersecurity expertise, (iv) the opportunity to perform Services for the Company, and (v) other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows.

This Agreement supplements and is incorporated into the Board Member Terms Memo dated February 22, 2026 ("Terms Memo"). In the event of conflict, this Agreement controls with respect to confidentiality and intellectual property matters.

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 Confidential Information.

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, in any form or medium (oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Business plans, financial projections, revenue data, and pricing strategies;
- Software, source code, algorithms, AI/ML models, training data, and system architectures;
- Security frameworks, compliance documentation, policies, and methodologies;
- Customer lists, prospect data, and partnership agreements;
- Product roadmaps, feature specifications, and technical documentation;
- Personnel information and compensation structures;
- Trade secrets as defined under the Colorado Uniform Trade Secrets Act (C.R.S. Section 7-74-102) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. Section 1839);
- Any information disclosed in board meetings, Discord channels, or written communications.

### 1.2 Exclusions.

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure without restriction;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;

(d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority (subject to Section 2.3 below).

The burden of proof to establish any exclusion rests with the Receiving Party. The fact that individual elements of Confidential Information are publicly available does not make a combination or compilation of such elements public.

### 1.3 Intellectual Property ("IP").

"IP" means any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, concepts, ideas, works of authorship, software, code, algorithms, designs, data, documentation, trade secrets, and any other intellectual property, whether or not patentable, copyrightable, or otherwise protectable.

### 1.4 Trade Secrets.

"Trade Secrets" means any information that: (i) derives independent economic value from not being generally known or readily ascertainable; and (ii) is subject to reasonable measures to maintain its secrecy, as defined under C.R.S. Section 7-74-102 and 18 U.S.C. Section 1839.

### 1.5 Services.

"Services" means all work performed by Knutsen for or on behalf of Project Green or the Company, whether as Board Member, independent contractor, consultant, or in any other capacity, including but not limited to advisory services under the Terms Memo and any separately contracted work engagements.

### 1.6 Pre-Existing IP.

"Pre-Existing IP" means any IP owned or developed by either Party prior to the Effective Date that is listed in Exhibit A (for Knutsen) or Exhibit B (for the Company) attached hereto.

## 2. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

### 2.1 Non-Disclosure.

Each Party, when acting as the Receiving Party, agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence using no less than reasonable care, and no less care than it uses to protect its own confidential information;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of furthering the objectives of Project Green, including the performance of Services;

(d) Not use Confidential Information for personal gain, competitive advantage, or any purpose outside the scope of the Project Green relationship;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or extract Confidential Information beyond what is strictly necessary for the stated purpose.

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosures.

Either Party may disclose the other Party's Confidential Information solely to:

(a) Legal counsel or professional advisors under a duty of confidentiality, and only to the extent necessary for legal advice;

(b) Government or regulatory bodies as required by law -- subject to the notice and cooperation obligations in Section 2.3.

### 2.3 Legal Compulsion.

If either Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information of the other Party, the compelled Party shall: (i) provide prompt prior written notice to the other Party (to the extent legally permissible) to enable that Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; (ii) cooperate reasonably with such efforts; and (iii) disclose only that portion of Confidential Information legally required, using commercially reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any disclosed portion.

### 2.4 Whistleblower & Government Reporting Carve-Out.

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, nothing herein shall prohibit either Party from: (i) reporting possible violations of law or regulation to any governmental authority, including the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), the Department of Justice, or any other federal or state agency; (ii) making disclosures protected under whistleblower provisions of applicable federal or state law, including the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (15 U.S.C. Section 78u-6); or (iii) filing a charge with or participating in any investigation or proceeding conducted by any governmental authority. Neither Party is required to notify the other prior to making any such report or disclosure.

### 2.5 Breach Notification.

Each Party shall notify the other promptly -- and in no event later than five (5) business days -- upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, loss, or misuse of the other Party's Confidential Information. Such notice shall describe the circumstances of the incident in reasonable detail and the steps being taken to mitigate harm.

### 2.6 Term & Survival.

Confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive termination of the Board Member relationship and any future change in the Parties' arrangement, as follows:

- **General Confidential Information:** obligations are indefinite.
- **Trade Secrets:** obligations are perpetual and shall remain in effect for so long as the information qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law, with no expiration.

### 2.7 Return or Destruction.

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of the Board Member relationship, the Receiving Party shall within ten (10) business days: (i) return all tangible Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party; (ii) destroy all copies, notes, and extracts in its possession and certify such destruction in writing upon request. One archival copy may be retained by counsel solely for legal compliance purposes, and such retained copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations hereunder.

### 2.8 DTSA Immunity Notice.

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. Section 1833(b)), each Party is hereby notified that:

(a) An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

(b) An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual (i) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (ii) does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.

## 3. Intellectual Property

This Section 3 governs IP created in connection with Project Green. The Parties acknowledge that the confidentiality obligations in Section 2 are mutual, while the IP assignment provisions below reflect the nature of the working relationship: Knutsen contributes work product to the Company, and the Company retains ownership of Project Green IP.

### 3.1 Pre-Existing IP.

Each Party has identified pre-existing IP relevant to the Company's business or operations in the applicable Exhibit: Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP is listed in Exhibit A, and the Company's Pre-Existing IP is described in Exhibit B.

Pre-Existing IP listed in the applicable Exhibit remains the sole property of the Party that owns it. If Knutsen incorporates any of his Pre-Existing IP into work product created for the Company, Knutsen hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, perpetual, irrevocable license to use, modify, and distribute such Pre-Existing IP, but only as incorporated into such Company work product.

Any IP relevant to the Company's business that is created by Knutsen and is not listed in Exhibit A is presumed to be newly created under this engagement and subject to Section 3.2.

### 3.2 Assignment of Knutsen's Contributions.

Any IP created, conceived, developed, or contributed by Knutsen in connection with the performance of Services -- including feedback, suggestions, technical guidance, code, designs, improvements, or inventions -- is hereby irrevocably assigned to the Company (or to Michael Leavenworth as Founder prior to formal incorporation). This assignment is worldwide, royalty-free, and covers all patent rights, copyright rights, trade secret rights, and all other intellectual property rights. Knutsen waives any moral rights in such contributions to the fullest extent permitted by law. For the avoidance of doubt, this assignment does not apply to Pre-Existing IP identified in Exhibit A, except to the extent of the license granted in Section 3.1.

Without limiting the foregoing, the Parties have identified specific contracted work-for-hire engagements in Exhibit C ("Contracted Work-for-Hire Schedule"). All deliverables produced under Exhibit C are works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law and are owned exclusively by the Company. To the extent any such deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire under applicable law, Knutsen hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such deliverable to the Company. Deliverables under Exhibit C are intended to be incorporated into Project Green's product and service offerings for the benefit of the Company's current and future customers and clients.

### 3.3 Company IP Ownership.

Leavenworth (and upon incorporation, the Company) retains all right, title, and interest in all Project Green IP, including the codebase, documentation, business plans, data models, and all derivative works. Nothing in this Agreement transfers any ownership interest in Company IP to Knutsen.

### 3.4 Further Assurances.

Knutsen agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, record, or enforce the Company's ownership of assigned IP, at no additional cost to Knutsen. If Knutsen fails to execute such documents within ten (10) business days of written request, Knutsen hereby appoints Leavenworth (or the Company) as attorney-in-fact, coupled with an interest, to execute such documents on Knutsen's behalf.

### 3.5 No License to Knutsen.

Nothing in this Agreement grants Knutsen any license, right, or interest in any IP owned by the Company or Leavenworth, except as expressly set forth in the Terms Memo or a separate written agreement.

### 3.6 Future Licensing.

The Parties acknowledge that future arrangements may include mutual licensing of IP between them (e.g., Knutsen licensing cybersecurity tools to the Company, or the Company licensing technology to Knutsen for separate business use). Any such licensing shall be governed by a separate written agreement and is not granted or implied by this Agreement.

### 3.7 Federal IP Protections.

The Parties acknowledge that Trade Secrets disclosed under this Agreement are protected under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. Section 1836 et seq.). In the event of willful or malicious misappropriation of Trade Secrets, the non-breaching Party may seek exemplary damages of up to two times (2x) actual damages and attorney's fees as provided under 18 U.S.C. Section 1836(b)(3) and C.R.S. Section 7-74-104.

## 4. Privacy

### 4.1 Personal Data.

Each Party agrees to handle any personal data of the other Party or of the Company's customers, employees, or contractors in compliance with applicable privacy laws, including but not limited to the Colorado Privacy Act (C.R.S. Section 6-1-1301 et seq.) and applicable federal law.

### 4.2 No Unauthorized Collection.

Neither Party shall collect, store, or process personal data obtained through the performance of Services or the Project Green relationship for any purpose other than furthering the objectives of Project Green.

## 5. Conflicts of Interest

Knutsen shall promptly disclose to Leavenworth any actual or potential conflict of interest that may arise from the performance of Services, including any engagement with a directly competing business. Given the advisory-only, non-fiduciary nature of the current role as described in the Terms Memo, this disclosure obligation is limited to conflicts that could reasonably impair Knutsen's ability to provide independent advice or perform Services. If Knutsen's role evolves beyond advisory (e.g., to officer, employee, or co-founder), the Parties agree to negotiate enhanced conflict-of-interest provisions appropriate to the expanded role.

## 6. Remedies

### 6.1 Injunctive Relief.

Each Party acknowledges that any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement by either Party may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the non-breaching Party is entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the requirement to post bond or prove actual damages, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity.

### 6.2 Damages.

In addition to injunctive relief, the non-breaching Party may recover all actual damages caused by any breach of this Agreement. In cases of willful or malicious misappropriation of Trade Secrets, exemplary damages of up to two times (2x) actual damages may be sought under the DTSA and Colorado UTSA.

### 6.3 Attorney's Fees.

In any legal proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorney's fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.

## 7. Good Faith & Shared Intent

### 7.1 Good Faith.

The Parties acknowledge that Project Green is an early-stage venture and that their working relationship is evolving. Both Parties agree to act in good faith toward one another in all matters arising under or related to this Agreement. This includes, without limitation, good faith in the interpretation of ambiguous provisions, the resolution of unforeseen circumstances, and the negotiation of future agreements as the relationship develops.

### 7.2 Shared Purpose.

The Parties share the common objective of building Project Green into a durable, high-value enterprise through the formation and growth of a Delaware C-Corporation. Both Parties agree to act in the best interests of the future Company and its long-term enterprise value. Decisions, contributions, and conduct under this Agreement should be guided by this shared purpose.

### 7.3 Pace of Development.

The Parties acknowledge that they are operating at startup speed with limited resources. This Agreement represents a good-faith first version of the legal framework governing their relationship. Both Parties commit to revisiting and formalizing this Agreement with qualified legal counsel as the Company matures -- including upon incorporation, upon any change in Knutsen's role (e.g., from advisor to officer, employee, or co-founder), or upon any equity grant or compensation change. Neither Party shall exploit ambiguities or gaps in this Agreement to the detriment of the other Party or the Company.

## 8. General Provisions

### 8.1 Governing Law & Jurisdiction.

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties consent to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state and federal courts located in Delaware for resolution of any dispute arising under this Agreement.

### 8.2 Entire Agreement.

Together with the Terms Memo dated February 22, 2026, this Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to confidentiality and IP, and supersedes all prior discussions, understandings, or agreements on these subjects.

### 8.3 Amendment.

This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both Parties.

### 8.4 Severability.

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid or unenforceable, that provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable, and the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

### 8.5 Waiver.

Failure by either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of future enforcement of that provision.

### 8.6 Counterparts & Electronic Signatures.

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid and binding to the same extent as original signatures under the Electronic Signatures in Global and National Commerce Act (E-SIGN Act, 15 U.S.C. Section 7001 et seq.) and applicable state law.

### 8.7 Notice.

Notices under this Agreement shall be delivered via email to the addresses on record between the Parties, with written confirmation of receipt.

### 8.8 Relationship of the Parties.

Nothing in this Agreement creates a partnership, joint venture, employment, or agency relationship. Knutsen remains an independent advisory Board Member as described in the Terms Memo, unless and until a separate written agreement establishes a different relationship.

### 8.9 Headings.

Section headings are for convenience only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

### 8.10 Assignment to Entity.

Upon incorporation of the Delaware C-Corporation, this Agreement shall be automatically assigned to and assumed by the Company, and all rights and obligations of Leavenworth hereunder shall vest in the Company. Knutsen consents to such assignment without further action. Following such assignment, references to "Leavenworth" in his capacity as Founder shall be deemed to refer to the Company where the context requires.
