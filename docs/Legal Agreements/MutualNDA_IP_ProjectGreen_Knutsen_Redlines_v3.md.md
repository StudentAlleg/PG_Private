## Preamble

This Mutual Non-Disclosure and IP Assignment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of March 6, 2026 ("Effective Date") by and between:

**Kristopher Knutsen** ("Knutsen"), an individual residing in the State of Colorado, serving as Board Member of Project Green; and

**Michael Leavenworth** ("Leavenworth"), Founder of Project Green, an individual residing in the State of Florida, acting on behalf of Project Green, a Delaware C-Corporation in formation ("Company").

Knutsen and Leavenworth are each referred to individually as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

Each Party anticipates disclosing Confidential Information to the other in connection with Project Green. In any given disclosure, the Party disclosing information is the "Disclosing Party" and the Party receiving information is the "Receiving Party." Both Parties are simultaneously bound by the obligations of both roles.

In consideration of (i) the mutual exchange of Confidential Information, (ii) Knutsen's continued appointment as Board Member under the Terms Memo, (iii) access to each other's Confidential Information including source code, business plans, technical documentation, and cybersecurity expertise, (iv) the opportunity to perform Services for the Company, and (v) other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are hereby acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows.

This Agreement supplements and is incorporated into the Board Member Terms Memo dated February 22, 2026 ("Terms Memo"). In the event of conflict, this Agreement controls with respect to confidentiality and intellectual property matters.

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 Confidential Information.

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, in any form or medium (oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Business plans, financial projections, revenue data, and pricing strategies;
- Software, source code, algorithms, AI/ML models, training data, and system architectures;
- Security frameworks, compliance documentation, policies, and methodologies;
- Customer lists, prospect data, and partnership agreements;
- Product roadmaps, feature specifications, and technical documentation;
- Personnel information and compensation structures;
- Trade secrets as defined under the Colorado Uniform Trade Secrets Act (C.R.S. §7-74-102) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1839);
- Written and verbal communications made in connection with Project Green, including through the Project Green Discord server, direct messages between Project Green members, and board meeting discussions.

> **[REDLINE — §1.1]** Revised "Any information disclosed in board meetings, Discord channels, or written communications" to explicitly name the Project Green Discord server and direct messages between Project Green members, scoped to communications made in connection with Project Green.
>
> **Reasoning:** Discord is the Parties' primary communication channel and must remain explicitly included — removing it entirely would have been an overcorrection. Direct messages between Project Green members are equally important, as significant business discussions (including codebase access, role discussions, and financial matters) occur there. The scoping language "in connection with Project Green" prevents the clause from sweeping in unrelated personal or casual Discord activity, while capturing all legitimate Project Green communications across all channels and formats.

### 1.2 Exclusions.

*(Unchanged)*

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure without restriction;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;

(d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority (subject to Section 2.3 below).

The burden of proof to establish any exclusion rests with the Receiving Party. The fact that individual elements of Confidential Information are publicly available does not make a combination or compilation of such elements public.

## 2. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

### 2.1 Non-Disclosure. *(Unchanged)*

Each Party, when acting as the Receiving Party, agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence using no less than reasonable care, and no less care than it uses to protect its own confidential information;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of furthering the objectives of Project Green, including the performance of Services;

(d) Not use Confidential Information for personal gain, competitive advantage, or any purpose outside the scope of the Project Green relationship;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or extract Confidential Information beyond what is strictly necessary for the stated purpose.

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosures.

Either Party may disclose the other Party's Confidential Information solely to:

(a) Legal counsel or professional advisors under a duty of confidentiality, and only to the extent necessary for legal advice;

(b) Employees, contractors, and professional advisors of either Party who have a legitimate need to know in furtherance of Project Green and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;

(c) Government or regulatory bodies as required by law — subject to the notice and cooperation obligations in Section 2.3.

> **[REDLINE — §2.2]** Added clause (b) permitting disclosure to employees, contractors, and professional advisors with a need to know.
>
> **Reasoning:** The original draft only permitted disclosure to legal counsel and government bodies. This is practically unworkable — both Knutsen and Leavenworth will inevitably need to discuss Project Green matters with developers, accountants, other advisors, or contractors. Without this clause, any such disclosure would technically be a breach. Industry standard NDA practice explicitly allows disclosure to personnel with a need to know who are bound by equivalent confidentiality. This clause includes a written confidentiality obligation requirement to protect both parties.

### 2.3 Legal Compulsion. *(Unchanged)*

### 2.4 Whistleblower & Government Reporting Carve-Out. *(Unchanged)*

### 2.5 Breach Notification. *(Unchanged)*

### 2.6 Term & Survival.

Confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive termination of the Board Member relationship and any future change in the Parties' arrangement, as follows:

- **General Confidential Information:** obligations shall remain in effect for five (5) years from the date of disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information, or five (5) years from the termination of this Agreement, whichever is later.
- **Trade Secrets:** obligations are perpetual and shall remain in effect for so long as the information qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law, with no expiration.

> **[REDLINE — §2.6]** Changed "General Confidential Information: obligations are indefinite" to a five (5) year term measured from the later of date of disclosure or termination of the Agreement.
>
> **Reasoning:** Indefinite confidentiality obligations on general (non-trade-secret) CI are legally aggressive and have been challenged as unenforceable in multiple jurisdictions. A defined 5-year term is the industry standard for startup and commercial NDAs and is actually more legally defensible than "indefinite." Critically, anything that qualifies as a Trade Secret under §1.4 remains perpetually protected — so the 5-year sunset only applies to general business information that loses its commercial sensitivity over time. This change protects both parties: the Company's truly sensitive information is still protected forever; the obligation to protect stale general business information has a reasonable sunset.

## 3. Intellectual Property

This Section 3 governs IP created in connection with Project Green. The Parties acknowledge that the confidentiality obligations in Section 2 are mutual, while the IP assignment provisions below reflect the nature of the working relationship: Knutsen contributes work product to the Company under Exhibit C, and the Company retains ownership of such Project Green IP.

> **[REDLINE — §3 intro]** Added "under Exhibit C" to the descriptive sentence to make clear that IP contributions are scoped to contracted deliverables, not open-ended.
>
> **Reasoning:** Sets the interpretive context for the entire IP section before any ambiguous language can be applied broadly.

### 3.1 Pre-Existing IP.

Each Party has identified pre-existing IP relevant to the Company's business or operations in the applicable Exhibit: Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP is listed in Exhibit A, and the Company's Pre-Existing IP is described in Exhibit B.

Pre-Existing IP listed in the applicable Exhibit remains the sole property of the Party that owns it. If Knutsen incorporates any of his Pre-Existing IP into work product created for the Company, Knutsen hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, modify, and distribute such Pre-Existing IP, but only as incorporated into such Company work product. This license shall survive termination of this Agreement solely with respect to Company work product already delivered and accepted prior to termination. In the event of a material breach of this Agreement by the Company that remains uncured thirty (30) days after written notice, Knutsen may terminate this license upon written notice, provided such termination shall not affect end-user rights in products already distributed to customers prior to such notice.

Any IP created by Knutsen that is not listed in Exhibit A and was created specifically at the written direction of the Company as a deliverable under Exhibit C is subject to Section 3.2. IP developed by Knutsen independently, outside the scope of Exhibit C, shall not be presumed to belong to the Company regardless of its relevance to the Company's business.

> **[REDLINE — §3.1, two changes]**
>
> **Change A:** Removed "perpetual" and "irrevocable" from the Pre-Existing IP license grant; replaced with a license that survives termination only for already-delivered work product, and is terminable on material breach with a customer carve-out.
>
> **Reasoning:** "Perpetual and irrevocable" means that even if Leavenworth materially breaches this Agreement — fails to pay, misappropriates Knutsen's CI, dissolves the Company — Knutsen has no ability to withdraw the license on his own IP. That is an unreasonable outcome. The revised language is commercially standard: the Company keeps the license for work product it has already received and distributed, but Knutsen retains leverage if the relationship breaks down badly. The customer carve-out protects the Company's end-users and makes this a reasonable ask, not an aggressive one.
>
> **Change B:** Replaced "Any IP relevant to the Company's business that is created by Knutsen and is not listed in Exhibit A is presumed to be newly created under this engagement and subject to Section 3.2" with language tying the assignment presumption to Exhibit C deliverables only.
>
> **Reasoning:** The original "relevant to the Company's business" standard is dangerously broad. An AI platform for accounting firms could credibly claim relevance to almost any work Knutsen does in technology, compliance, finance, or AI. The revised language ties the assignment presumption specifically to deliverables created at the Company's written direction under Exhibit C — the things Knutsen was actually contracted and paid to build. Anything Knutsen develops independently, on his own time, stays his regardless of whether it's in the same industry.

### 3.2 Assignment of Knutsen's Contributions.

Any IP created, developed, or contributed by Knutsen specifically at the written direction of the Company in connection with the performance of Services under Exhibit C, and actually delivered to the Company in tangible form, is hereby irrevocably assigned to the Company (or to Michael Leavenworth as Founder prior to formal incorporation). This assignment is worldwide, royalty-free, and covers all patent rights, copyright rights, trade secret rights, and all other intellectual property rights. Knutsen waives any moral rights in such contributions to the fullest extent permitted by law. For the avoidance of doubt, this assignment does not apply to Pre-Existing IP identified in Exhibit A, except to the extent of the license granted in Section 3.1.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, this assignment shall not apply to any IP that Knutsen develops: (i) entirely on his own time; (ii) without use of the Company's Confidential Information, equipment, or resources; and (iii) without direct use of work product created for the Company under this Agreement. Knutsen shall have the burden of demonstrating that any disputed IP meets all three criteria.

The Parties have identified specific contracted work-for-hire engagements in Exhibit C ("Contracted Work-for-Hire Schedule"). All deliverables produced under Exhibit C are works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law and are owned exclusively by the Company. To the extent any such deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire under applicable law, Knutsen hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such deliverable to the Company.

> **[REDLINE — §3.2, two changes]**
>
> **Change A:** Replaced "created, conceived, developed, or contributed by Knutsen in connection with the performance of Services" with "created, developed, or contributed by Knutsen specifically at the written direction of the Company in connection with the performance of Services under Exhibit C, and actually delivered to the Company in tangible form."
>
> **Reasoning:** "Conceived" is the most legally dangerous word in the original draft. An idea Knutsen has while reading a Project Green document — but never writes down, never delivers, never develops — could be claimed as assigned to the Company under the original language. Removing "conceived" and adding "actually delivered in tangible form" means assignment only triggers on real, deliverable work product. Adding "written direction" and "under Exhibit C" further limits this to work the Company actually requested and paid for.
>
> **Change B:** Added the personal time / independent work carve-out paragraph.
>
> **Reasoning:** Neither Delaware (governing law) nor Colorado (Knutsen's state) provides statutory protection for IP developed by contractors on personal time — no statute here automatically shields independent work from assignment. Without this explicit carve-out, the "in connection with Services" language combined with the broad Services definition could sweep in work Knutsen does on his own time that touches adjacent technology, compliance, or AI territory. The three-part test (own time, no Company resources, no Company work product) is the standard formulation used in startup contractor agreements. Knutsen bears the burden of proof, which is commercially fair to Leavenworth.

### 3.3 Company IP Ownership. *(Unchanged)*

### 3.4 Further Assurances.

Knutsen agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, record, or enforce the Company's ownership of assigned IP, at no additional cost to Knutsen. If Knutsen fails to execute such documents within ten (10) business days of written request, Knutsen hereby appoints Leavenworth (or the Company) as attorney-in-fact, coupled with an interest, solely to execute IP assignment and registration documents directly related to New IP created and delivered under Exhibit C of this Agreement. This power of attorney does not extend to Pre-Existing IP listed in Exhibit A, IP developed by Knutsen on his own time under Section 3.2, or any IP developed outside the scope of Exhibit C.

> **[REDLINE — §3.4]** Added "solely to execute IP assignment and registration documents directly related to New IP created and delivered under Exhibit C" and the explicit exclusions paragraph.
>
> **Reasoning:** The original grant of attorney-in-fact authority had no scope limitation. As written, Leavenworth could theoretically execute any document on Knutsen's behalf — including documents purporting to assign Pre-Existing IP from Exhibit A or IP Knutsen developed independently. The revised language limits this power precisely to its legitimate purpose: getting formal IP registrations completed for contracted Exhibit C deliverables that Knutsen has already been paid to create. The explicit carve-outs for Exhibit A IP and independently-developed IP close any ambiguity.

### 3.5 No License to Knutsen. *(Unchanged)*

### 3.6 Future Licensing. *(Unchanged)*

### 3.7 Federal IP Protections. *(Unchanged)*

### 3.8 Compensation for Additional IP Contributions. *(New Section)*

To the extent the Company requests Knutsen to create, contribute, or assign IP beyond the deliverables identified in Exhibit C, any such contribution shall be subject to separate written compensation negotiation prior to Knutsen undertaking such work. No additional IP contribution shall be deemed made under this Agreement without a signed written addendum specifying the scope of work and compensation. The revenue share set forth in the Terms Memo does not constitute compensation for IP contributions beyond Exhibit C.

> **[REDLINE — §3.8 NEW]** Added new section establishing that IP contributions beyond Exhibit C require separate written compensation negotiation.
>
> **Reasoning:** The Terms Memo provides Knutsen a 0.25% revenue share as compensation for his advisory role as Board Member — not as payment for building IP. The current agreement, combined with the broad "in connection with Services" language, could be read to mean that any IP Knutsen contributes (beyond Exhibit C) is assigned away for no additional consideration beyond his advisory revenue share. If Michael asks Knutsen to build something substantial — a compliance module, a security framework, additional research — that should be a separate paid engagement with a separate agreement. This clause makes that explicit and prevents retroactive arguments that the revenue share covered everything.
