## Gaps and Loose Ends

### 1. Exhibit A Item #3 Is "[Reserved]"

Kris has a placeholder third entry in his Pre-Existing IP schedule: "[Reserved — additional items to be listed by Knutsen prior to execution]." This should be filled in with specific items or struck before signing. An open-ended reservation creates ambiguity — if a dispute arises later, Kris could argue something was intended for that slot. The agreement's own language (§3.1) states that anything not listed in Exhibit A and not created under Exhibit C is presumed to be Kris's independent IP, so the reserved slot is arguably redundant, but it's cleaner to resolve it explicitly.

**Action:** Ask Kris to fill in or strike item #3 before execution.

### 2. Exhibit C Payment Terms Are "To Be Agreed Separately"

The Exhibit C work-for-hire schedule specifies 6 hours at $70/hr ($420 total), but states "Payment terms and delivery schedule to be agreed separately." The entire IP assignment framework in v3 now hinges on Exhibit C as the trigger for assignment. If Kris begins work before payment terms are locked down, there is a question of whether the work was performed "under Exhibit C" or informally — which could complicate the assignment chain.

**Action:** Agree on payment terms (timing, method, milestones) before or at signing, even if informally in a brief written exchange.

### 3. No Dispute Resolution Short of Litigation

Section 8.1 sends all disputes directly to Delaware state and federal courts. For two individuals in an early-stage startup with a $420 contracted work scope, Delaware litigation is expensive and disproportionate. A mediation-first clause (e.g., "the Parties shall attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute through mediation before initiating litigation") would be more practical, less costly, and more consistent with the collaborative spirit expressed in Section 7. This would protect both parties equally.

**Action:** Consider proposing a mutual addition of a mediation-first step to §8.1. This is not a redline reopening — it benefits both parties.

### 4. "Board Member" Has No Formal Corporate Governance Meaning Yet

The Company is not yet incorporated. The agreement references Kris's role as "Board Member" throughout, but there is no formal board, no corporate resolutions, and no governance structure. The Terms Memo (February 22, 2026) may define what "Board Member" means in this pre-incorporation context, but the NDA itself does not. Once the Delaware C-Corp forms, Kris's board seat should be formalized through proper corporate resolutions, a board consent, and (if applicable) a D&O indemnification agreement.

**Action:** No immediate action required — this resolves naturally at incorporation. Flag for post-incorporation corporate housekeeping.

### 5. Agreement Is Silent on Non-Incorporation Scenario

Section 8.10 handles assignment of the agreement to the C-Corp upon incorporation. However, the agreement does not address what happens if the entity never forms. In that scenario, all Company obligations rest on Michael Leavenworth personally as Founder (per the Notes section: "Prior to formal incorporation of the Delaware C-Corp, 'Company' obligations vest in and are enforceable by Michael Leavenworth personally as Founder"). This is probably acceptable given current reality, but it means Mike carries personal liability for all Company-side obligations indefinitely until incorporation occurs.

**Action:** Understand and accept the personal liability exposure. Incorporation resolves this. No amendment needed unless incorporation timeline extends significantly.

### 6. No Indemnification Clause

Neither party indemnifies the other against third-party claims arising from the relationship. At this stage and scope (advisory board member, $420 contracted work), this is typical and proportionate. However, if the relationship scales — particularly if Kris takes on an operational role, handles customer data, or delivers security frameworks used in production — indemnification provisions should be added in whatever agreement supersedes this one.

**Action:** No immediate action. Flag for inclusion in any future expanded agreement.
