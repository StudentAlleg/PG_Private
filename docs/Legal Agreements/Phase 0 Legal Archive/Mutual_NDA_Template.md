## 1. PURPOSE

The Parties wish to explore a business relationship related to the development
of automation tooling for cloud accounting systems, AI-driven data integration,
and related technology services (the "Purpose"). In connection with the Purpose,
each Party may disclose Confidential Information (as defined below) to the other
Party. This Agreement sets forth the terms under which such Confidential
Information will be disclosed and protected.

## 2. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means any and all non-public information disclosed by
either Party to the other Party, whether orally, in writing, electronically, or
by any other means, including but not limited to:

(a) **Technical Information**: Source code, software architecture, system
designs, algorithms, databases, data schemas, APIs, prototypes, tools, and
technical documentation;

(b) **Business Information**: Business plans, strategies, financial models,
pricing, customer lists, client data, vendor relationships, market research,
go-to-market plans, and roadmaps;

(c) **Financial Data**: Accounting records, journal entries, ledger data,
invoices, payroll information, tax records, billing data, and any financial
information belonging to either Party or their clients;

(d) **Client Data**: Any information pertaining to clients of either Party,
including but not limited to client identities, financial records, account
information, and business operations;

(e) **Trade Secrets**: Any information that derives economic value from not
being generally known, including proprietary processes, methods, techniques,
and know-how;

(f) **Project Materials**: All materials shared via Discord, email, file
transfer, screen share, or any other communication channel related to the
Purpose, including project management artifacts, specifications, and
documentation;

(g) **Personnel Information**: Information about employees, contractors,
advisors, and their roles, compensation, or capabilities.

Confidential Information includes all copies, summaries, analyses, and
derivative works based on disclosed information, regardless of who prepared them.

## 3. EXCLUSIONS FROM CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Was publicly available at the time of disclosure, or becomes publicly
available through no fault of the Receiving Party;

(b) Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure, as
demonstrated by written records predating the disclosure;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or
reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information, as demonstrated
by written records;

(d) Is lawfully received from a third party without restriction on disclosure
and without breach of any obligation of confidentiality;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided
that the Receiving Party gives the Disclosing Party prompt written notice of
such requirement (to the extent legally permitted) and cooperates with the
Disclosing Party's efforts to seek a protective order or other appropriate
remedy.

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF THE RECEIVING PARTY

Each Party, when receiving Confidential Information (the "Receiving Party"),
agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and protect it with
at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential
information, but in no event less than reasonable care;

(b) Use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose described in Section 1
and for no other purpose;

(c) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior
written consent of the Disclosing Party, except to the Receiving Party's
employees, contractors, or legal advisors who (i) have a need to know for the
Purpose, and (ii) are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as
protective as those in this Agreement;

(d) Not reverse-engineer, decompile, or disassemble any software, prototypes,
or technical materials disclosed as Confidential Information;

(e) Not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as reasonably
necessary for the Purpose;

(f) Promptly notify the Disclosing Party of any unauthorized use or disclosure
of Confidential Information;

(g) Not use Confidential Information to compete with the Disclosing Party or
to solicit the Disclosing Party's clients, customers, or business
relationships.

## 5. HANDLING OF SENSITIVE FINANCIAL AND CLIENT DATA

Given that the Purpose involves access to financial and client data, both
Parties additionally agree to:

(a) Handle all financial and client data in accordance with applicable data
protection laws and industry standards, including but not limited to HIPAA
awareness guidelines and NIST security frameworks;

(b) Never store credentials, passwords, API keys, or access tokens in
plaintext, source code, or unencrypted files;

(c) Maintain appropriate technical safeguards (encryption in transit and at
rest, access controls, secure storage) when handling financial or client data;

(d) Not commingle client data of one party with data of the other party or
any third party;

(e) Immediately notify the other Party upon discovery of any data breach or
security incident involving Confidential Information.

## 6. NON-SOLICITATION

During the term of this Agreement and for a period of twelve (12) months
following its termination, neither Party shall, directly or indirectly:

(a) Solicit, recruit, or hire any employee, contractor, or advisor of the
other Party who was involved in the Purpose;

(b) Solicit or divert any client, customer, or business relationship of the
other Party that was made known through the disclosure of Confidential
Information.

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

(a) **Term**: This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of two (2)
years from the Effective Date, unless terminated earlier by either Party.

(b) **Termination**: Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time by
providing thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party.

(c) **Survival**: The obligations of confidentiality set forth in this
Agreement shall survive termination for a period of three (3) years from the
date of termination, or for as long as the Confidential Information remains a
trade secret under applicable law, whichever is longer.

## 8. RETURN OR DESTRUCTION OF MATERIALS

Upon termination of this Agreement or upon written request by the Disclosing
Party:

(a) The Receiving Party shall promptly return or destroy all Confidential
Information in its possession, including all copies, summaries, notes, and
derivative works;

(b) The Receiving Party shall provide written confirmation of such return or
destruction within fourteen (14) days of the request;

(c) Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain copies of
Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or
regulation, or as part of routine electronic backup systems, provided that such
retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this
Agreement.

## 9. NO LICENSE OR TRANSFER OF RIGHTS

Nothing in this Agreement grants either Party any license, ownership, or other
rights in or to the other Party's Confidential Information, intellectual
property, trade secrets, or proprietary technology, except the limited right to
use Confidential Information for the Purpose as described herein.

## 10. NO WARRANTY

All Confidential Information is provided "AS IS." Neither Party makes any
warranty, express or implied, regarding the accuracy, completeness, or fitness
for a particular purpose of any Confidential Information disclosed under this
Agreement.

## 11. REMEDIES

(a) Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause
irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages would be
an inadequate remedy.

(b) In the event of a breach or threatened breach, the Disclosing Party shall
be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, and any other
equitable remedies, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in
equity.

(c) The prevailing Party in any action to enforce this Agreement shall be
entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.

## 12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

(a) **Governing Law**: This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in
accordance with the laws of the State of _______________, without regard to
its conflicts of law principles.

(b) **Entire Agreement**: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement
between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes
all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements relating thereto.

(c) **Amendments**: This Agreement may only be amended by a written instrument
signed by both Parties.

(d) **Assignment**: Neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior
written consent of the other Party.

(e) **Severability**: If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid
or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and
effect.

(f) **Waiver**: The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this
Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of that Party's right to enforce that
provision or any other provision in the future.

(g) **Counterparts**: This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of
which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute
one and the same instrument.

(h) **Notices**: All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and
delivered to the addresses set forth above, or to such other address as either
Party may designate in writing.
