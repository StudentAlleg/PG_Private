## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, in any form or medium (oral, written, electronic, or otherwise), that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information includes, without limitation:

- Business plans, financial projections, revenue data, and pricing strategies;
- Software, source code, algorithms, AI/ML models, training data, and system architectures;
- Security frameworks, compliance documentation, policies, and methodologies;
- Customer lists, prospect data, and partnership agreements;
- Product roadmaps, feature specifications, and technical documentation;
- Personnel information and compensation structures;
- Personal information and financial information of either Party disclosed in connection with Project Green;
- Trade secrets as defined under the Colorado Uniform Trade Secrets Act (C.R.S. §7-74-102) and the federal Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1839);
- Written and verbal communications made in connection with Project Green, including through the Project Green Discord server, direct messages between Project Green members, and board meeting discussions.

The Parties agree that information shared in advisory board meetings, whether oral, written, or electronic, shall be presumed confidential unless expressly designated as non-confidential by the Disclosing Party.

> **AMENDMENT §1.1:** Added personal information of either Party, and added the presumption that board meeting information is confidential unless designated otherwise. Both additions protect both parties — Leavenworth's personal financial information shared during advisory discussions, and Knutsen's personal information shared in connection with the relationship.

### 1.2 Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party;

(b) Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure without restriction;

(c) Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;

(d) Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure;

(e) Is required to be disclosed by applicable law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority (subject to Section 2.3 below).

The burden of proof to establish any exclusion rests with the Receiving Party. The fact that individual elements of Confidential Information are publicly available does not make a combination or compilation of such elements public.

### 1.3 Intellectual Property ("IP")

"IP" means any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, concepts, ideas, works of authorship, software, code, algorithms, designs, data, documentation, trade secrets, and any other intellectual property, whether or not patentable, copyrightable, or otherwise protectable.

### 1.4 Trade Secrets

"Trade Secrets" means any information that: (i) derives independent economic value from not being generally known or readily ascertainable; and (ii) is subject to reasonable measures to maintain its secrecy, as defined under C.R.S. §7-74-102 and 18 U.S.C. §1839. For purposes of this Agreement, information shall only be treated as a Trade Secret if it meets the statutory requirements at the time enforcement is sought.

> **AMENDMENT §1.4:** Added "at the time enforcement is sought." This prevents either party from claiming trade secret protection (and perpetual confidentiality) over information that no longer qualifies — e.g., because it became public or the owner stopped taking reasonable measures to protect it. Both parties benefit from a clear, enforceable standard.

### 1.5 Services

"Services" means all work performed by Knutsen for or on behalf of Project Green or the Company, whether as Board Member, independent contractor, consultant, or in any other capacity, including but not limited to advisory services under the Terms Memo and any separately contracted work engagements identified in Exhibit C.

### 1.6 Pre-Existing IP

"Pre-Existing IP" means any IP owned or developed by a Party prior to the Effective Date that is listed in Exhibit A (Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP) or Exhibit B (Company's Pre-Existing IP), as applicable.

### 1.7 Residual Knowledge

"Residual Knowledge" means general ideas, concepts, know-how, methodologies, and techniques that are retained in the unaided memory of a person who has had access to Confidential Information, provided that such person has not intentionally memorized specific Confidential Information for the purpose of retaining and subsequently using or disclosing it. Neither Party's confidentiality obligations under this Agreement restrict the use of Residual Knowledge; however, this Section does not grant a license under any patent, copyright, or other intellectual property right, nor does it permit the disclosure of specific, identifiable Confidential Information such as financial figures, customer names, pricing terms, source code, or proprietary formulas.

> **AMENDMENT §1.7 — New Section:** Added Residual Knowledge definition. Both Knutsen and Leavenworth engage in multiple professional relationships. Without this clause, general knowledge gained during the advisory relationship — strategic patterns, architectural concepts, industry insights — could be claimed as CI by either party, effectively preventing the other from operating in adjacent fields. The clause is tightly bounded: it does not permit disclosure of *specific* CI (financial figures, customer names, source code, etc.) and does not grant any IP license. It protects both parties' ability to use general professional knowledge while maintaining strict protection over specific confidential details.

### 1.8 General Strategic Advice

For the avoidance of doubt, Confidential Information does not include general strategic advice, recommendations, or observations provided by either Party that reflect that Party's cumulative professional experience and judgment, even where such advice may be informed by patterns observed across multiple advisory engagements, provided that such advice does not reveal specific, identifiable Confidential Information of either Party.

> **AMENDMENT §1.8 — New Section:** Added General Strategic Advice exclusion. Knutsen's primary value to Project Green is strategic advice drawn from his cybersecurity and compliance expertise. Leavenworth's primary value to any future advisory relationship is strategic advice drawn from his accounting and AI expertise. Without this clause, either party's general advice could be claimed as CI by the other, which would chill the advisory relationship and undermine its purpose. The exclusion is narrow: it does not protect advice that reveals *specific* CI — only general strategic guidance drawn from cumulative experience.

## 2. Mutual Confidentiality Obligations

### 2.1 Non-Disclosure

Each Party, when acting as the Receiving Party, agrees to:

(a) Hold all Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party in strict confidence using no less than reasonable care, and no less care than it uses to protect its own confidential information;

(b) Not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party;

(c) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of furthering the objectives of Project Green, including the performance of Services;

(d) Not use Confidential Information for personal gain, competitive advantage, or any purpose outside the scope of the Project Green relationship;

(e) Not copy, reproduce, or extract Confidential Information beyond what is strictly necessary for the stated purpose;

(f) Not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, structure, or design of any Confidential Information, including software, algorithms, or technical systems.

> **AMENDMENT §2.1(f) — New Clause:** Added reverse engineering prohibition. Under U.S. law, reverse engineering is generally permitted unless contractually prohibited. Both parties share technical systems and architectures with each other — Knutsen's security frameworks, Leavenworth's AI platform. Neither party should reverse engineer the other's technical IP. This is standard in NDAs involving technical information.

### 2.2 Permitted Disclosures

Either Party may disclose the other Party's Confidential Information solely to:

(a) Legal counsel, accountants, or financial advisors retained by the Receiving Party who are under a professional or contractual duty of confidentiality, and only to the extent necessary for legal, accounting, or financial advice;

(b) Employees and contractors of either Party who have a legitimate need to know in furtherance of Project Green and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those in this Agreement;

(c) Government or regulatory bodies as required by law — subject to the notice and cooperation obligations in Section 2.3.

For the avoidance of doubt, "permitted recipients" under clause (b) does not include investors, portfolio company personnel, or other advisory board members unless the Disclosing Party provides prior written consent. The Receiving Party shall identify any permitted recipient to the Disclosing Party upon reasonable written request and shall remain responsible for any breach of this Agreement by such recipient.

> **AMENDMENT §2.2:** Clarified that "permitted recipients" in clause (b) uses the tightened language from Knutsen's v3 redline (employees/contractors with written confidentiality), and added explicit exclusion of investors, portfolio company personnel, and other advisory board members. Also added representative identification upon request and flow-down responsibility. Both parties benefit: Knutsen's CI is protected from casual sharing with Project Green's future investors, and Leavenworth's CI is protected from sharing with Knutsen's other business contacts.

### 2.3 Legal Compulsion

If either Party is required by law, regulation, court order, or governmental authority to disclose any Confidential Information of the other Party, the compelled Party shall: (i) provide prompt prior written notice to the other Party (to the extent legally permissible) to enable that Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; (ii) cooperate reasonably with such efforts; and (iii) disclose only that portion of Confidential Information legally required, using commercially reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment for any disclosed portion.

### 2.4 Whistleblower & Government Reporting Carve-Out

Notwithstanding any other provision of this Agreement, nothing herein shall prohibit either Party from: (i) reporting possible violations of law or regulation to any governmental authority, including the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), the Department of Justice, or any other federal or state agency; (ii) making disclosures protected under whistleblower provisions of applicable federal or state law, including the Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act (15 U.S.C. §78u-6) and the Colorado Whistleblower, Anti-Retaliation, Notice, and Independence in Health Care Act; or (iii) filing a charge with or participating in any investigation or proceeding conducted by any governmental authority. Neither Party is required to notify the other prior to making any such report or disclosure.

> **AMENDMENT §2.4:** Added reference to Colorado whistleblower statute alongside federal Dodd-Frank. Since Knutsen resides in Colorado, Colorado whistleblower protections apply to him regardless of Delaware choice of law. Explicit reference avoids ambiguity about which state protections are preserved.

### 2.5 Breach Notification

Each Party shall notify the other Party promptly — and in no event later than five (5) business days — upon becoming aware of any actual or suspected unauthorized disclosure, loss, or misuse of the other Party's Confidential Information. Such notice shall describe in reasonable detail: (i) the circumstances of the incident, (ii) the categories of Confidential Information affected, and (iii) the steps being taken to mitigate harm. The notifying Party shall cooperate reasonably with the other Party's investigation.

> **AMENDMENT §2.5:** Added detail requirements to breach notification — categories of CI affected, steps to mitigate, and cooperation obligation. The original only required notice of the incident. Both parties benefit from knowing *what* was compromised and *what is being done about it*.

### 2.6 Term & Survival

Confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive termination of the Board Member relationship and any future change in the Parties' arrangement, as follows:

**General Confidential Information:** obligations shall remain in effect for five (5) years from the date of disclosure of the applicable Confidential Information, or five (5) years from the termination of this Agreement, whichever is later.

**Trade Secrets:** obligations shall remain in effect for so long as the information qualifies as a Trade Secret under applicable law, with no fixed expiration. If the information ceases to qualify as a Trade Secret under §1.4, the five-year survival period for General Confidential Information shall apply from the date the information ceased to qualify.

> **AMENDMENT §2.6:** Added Trade Secret fallback clause. If information stops qualifying as a trade secret (e.g., the owner stops taking reasonable measures to protect it, or it becomes publicly known), the perpetual obligation steps down to the 5-year general CI term measured from when it stopped qualifying. This prevents either party from claiming perpetual protection over information that no longer meets the statutory trade secret standard — a gap that could otherwise be exploited.

### 2.7 Return or Destruction

Upon written request by the Disclosing Party or upon termination of the Board Member relationship, the Receiving Party shall within ten (10) business days: (i) return all tangible Confidential Information; (ii) permanently delete all electronic copies within the Receiving Party's reasonable control, including from local devices, cloud storage, and collaboration platforms; and (iii) certify such return and deletion in writing upon request. The Receiving Party acknowledges that copies may exist in automated backup systems, shared communication platforms (such as Discord), or email archives not within the Receiving Party's sole control; provided that: (a) the Receiving Party shall not intentionally access or use such copies for any purpose, and (b) any copies retained in such systems remain subject to all confidentiality obligations under this Agreement. One archival copy may be retained by counsel solely for legal compliance purposes, and such retained copy remains subject to all confidentiality obligations hereunder.

> **AMENDMENT §2.7:** Modernized return/destruction for digital reality. The original required "destroy all copies, notes, and extracts in its possession" — language from the paper-document era. In 2026, CI exists in Discord history, cloud backups, email archives, and synced devices. True destruction of all digital copies is practically impossible and courts recognize this. The revised language: (1) limits the obligation to copies within the party's reasonable control, (2) explicitly acknowledges automated backups and shared platforms exist, (3) prohibits intentional access to backup copies, and (4) maintains confidentiality obligations on any retained copies. Both parties' primary communication channel is Discord — this clause addresses that reality.

### 2.8 DTSA Immunity Notice

Pursuant to the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1833(b)), each Party is hereby notified that:

(a) An individual shall not be held criminally or civilly liable under any Federal or State trade secret law for the disclosure of a trade secret that is made (i) in confidence to a Federal, State, or local government official, either directly or indirectly, or to an attorney, and solely for the purpose of reporting or investigating a suspected violation of law; or (ii) in a complaint or other document filed in a lawsuit or other proceeding, if such filing is made under seal.

(b) An individual who files a lawsuit for retaliation by an employer for reporting a suspected violation of law may disclose the trade secret to the attorney of the individual and use the trade secret information in the court proceeding, if the individual (i) files any document containing the trade secret under seal; and (ii) does not disclose the trade secret, except pursuant to court order.

### 2.9 Scope of Disclosure

The Company shall limit Knutsen's access to Confidential Information to that which is reasonably necessary for Knutsen's advisory role. Knutsen shall not be deemed to have received Confidential Information solely by virtue of having access to general communication channels, repositories, or collaboration platforms (such as Discord) unless Knutsen actually accesses specific Confidential Information therein that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential. The Company shall not provide Knutsen with access to systems, channels, or repositories beyond those reasonably necessary for the advisory engagement without Knutsen's prior consent.

> **AMENDMENT §2.9 — New Section:** Added Scope of Disclosure. Without this clause, the Company could add Knutsen to every Discord channel, Slack workspace, and code repository — then later claim that everything Knutsen passively saw is CI he cannot use or discuss elsewhere. This "firehose" pattern is a known tactic for locking advisors into exclusive relationships. The clause requires the Company to limit access to what is necessary for the advisory role and ensures that passive channel access does not automatically create CI obligations. This protects Knutsen while encouraging responsible information sharing by both parties.

### 2.10 Information Barriers

Knutsen acknowledges that serving on multiple advisory boards or engaging in multiple ventures creates a risk of inadvertent cross-pollination of Confidential Information. Knutsen agrees to maintain reasonable information barriers to prevent the Company's Confidential Information from being disclosed to, or used for the benefit of, any other entity. Knutsen shall not disclose or use the Company's specific Confidential Information in connection with any other advisory engagement or business venture.

> **AMENDMENT §2.10 — New Section:** Added Information Barriers obligation. This is the complement to the Residual Knowledge clause in §1.7: while general knowledge is freely usable, *specific* CI must be siloed. This affirmative commitment by Knutsen demonstrates good faith to the Company and strengthens both parties' position if a dispute arises about cross-contamination.

### 2.11 Oral Disclosures

Confidential Information disclosed orally or visually shall be identified as confidential at the time of disclosure and summarized in writing by the Disclosing Party within ten (10) business days. Failure by the Disclosing Party to provide a written summary within this period does not waive protection if the information would be clearly recognized as confidential by a reasonable person familiar with the advisory context. The following categories of information shall always be deemed Confidential Information regardless of marking or written follow-up: (a) financial statements and projections, (b) customer and client lists, (c) pricing and compensation structures, (d) product roadmaps and unreleased features, (e) personnel information, and (f) source code and system architecture.

> **AMENDMENT §2.11 — New Section:** Added Oral Disclosure documentation procedure. Much of the Parties' CI exchange happens verbally — board meetings, Discord voice calls, phone conversations. Without a written follow-up mechanism, disputes about what was said become he-said/she-said. The 10-day written summary requirement creates a documentation trail while the auto-confidential categories ensure that the most sensitive information types are always protected regardless of whether anyone remembered to say "this is confidential" during a call. Both parties benefit equally.

### 2.12 Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning

Neither Party shall input, upload, or otherwise process the other Party's Confidential Information using any publicly available or consumer-grade generative artificial intelligence, large language model, or similar machine learning system, unless: (i) the system is deployed within the Receiving Party's own controlled infrastructure or under an enterprise agreement that prohibits use of input data for model training; (ii) the Disclosing Party has given prior written consent; and (iii) the Receiving Party takes commercially reasonable steps to prevent the Confidential Information from being retained, trained on, or disclosed by the AI system. For the avoidance of doubt, derived artifacts — including embeddings, vector representations, model weights, summaries, and log files — generated from Confidential Information are themselves Confidential Information and subject to all obligations under this Agreement.

> **AMENDMENT §2.12 — New Section:** Added AI/ML prohibition. This is a critical gap in v3 that affects both parties daily. Leavenworth uses AI coding assistants (Claude Code) to build Project Green. Knutsen uses AI tools (Grok, ChatGPT) for research. Without this clause, either party could input the other's CI into a consumer AI tool where it may be used for model training, stored in logs, or become accessible to other users. The clause permits use of enterprise/self-hosted AI systems that don't train on input data (protecting both parties' workflows) while prohibiting consumer-grade tools. The derived-artifacts provision ensures that embeddings, summaries, and logs generated from CI receive the same protection as the CI itself.

## 3. Intellectual Property

This Section 3 governs IP created in connection with Project Green. The Parties acknowledge that the confidentiality obligations in Section 2 are mutual and symmetric, while the IP assignment provisions below reflect the nature of the working relationship: Knutsen contributes work product to the Company under Exhibit C, and the Company retains ownership of such Project Green IP.

### 3.1 Pre-Existing IP

Each Party has identified pre-existing IP relevant to the Company's business or operations in the applicable Exhibit: Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP is listed in Exhibit A, and the Company's Pre-Existing IP is described in Exhibit B. Pre-Existing IP listed in the applicable Exhibit remains the sole property of the Party that owns it.

If Knutsen incorporates any of his Pre-Existing IP into work product created for the Company, Knutsen hereby grants the Company a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free license to use, modify, and distribute such Pre-Existing IP, but only as incorporated into such Company work product. This license shall survive termination of this Agreement solely with respect to Company work product already delivered and accepted prior to termination. In the event of a Material Breach of this Agreement by the Company that remains uncured thirty (30) days after written notice, Knutsen may terminate this license upon written notice, provided such termination shall not affect end-user rights in products already distributed to customers prior to such notice.

For purposes of this Section, "Material Breach" means: (a) misappropriation of Knutsen's Confidential Information or Pre-Existing IP; (b) use of Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP outside the scope of the license granted herein; or (c) failure to pay undisputed compensation owed under Exhibit C within thirty (30) days of Knutsen's written invoice. Breach of general provisions (including Sections 7 and 8) shall not, standing alone, constitute a Material Breach for purposes of license termination under this Section.

> **AMENDMENT §3.1 — Material Breach Definition:** Added a definition of "Material Breach" specific to license termination. The v3 language introduced a valuable right for Knutsen (license termination on material uncured breach) but left "material breach" undefined. Without a definition, any disagreement — informal communication lapses, delayed incorporation timeline, failure to update the agreement post-C-Corp formation — could arguably be "material." The definition limits the license termination trigger to its legitimate purpose: protecting Knutsen against misappropriation of his IP or nonpayment. General performance failures (Section 7 good faith, Section 8 general provisions) cannot trigger the nuclear option of license revocation. This protects the Company from disproportionate risk while preserving Knutsen's meaningful leverage against the three most serious breach categories.

For the avoidance of doubt, if the Company develops IP that is substantially derived from or based upon Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP disclosed in connection with advisory Services — as distinct from publicly available standards, frameworks, or methodologies — such development shall be treated as an incorporation of Pre-Existing IP for purposes of this Section, and the license terms herein shall apply.

> **AMENDMENT §3.1 — Conceptual Derivatives:** Added provision addressing the gap between literal incorporation and conceptual adoption. Without this clause, the Company could observe Knutsen's security framework in a board meeting, build a similar framework into Project Green using the same concepts but different code, and argue that no "incorporation" occurred — thereby avoiding the license altogether while benefiting from Knutsen's Pre-Existing IP. The clause ties the license trigger to substantial derivation, not literal copying, while explicitly excluding concepts drawn from public standards (NIST, HIPAA, SOC frameworks) that neither party owns. This protects Knutsen's pre-existing expertise while preserving the Company's right to implement industry-standard practices independently.

Any IP created by Knutsen that is not listed in Exhibit A and was created specifically at the written direction of the Company as a deliverable under Exhibit C is subject to Section 3.2. IP developed by Knutsen independently, outside the scope of Exhibit C, shall not be presumed to belong to the Company regardless of its relevance to the Company's business.

### 3.2 Assignment of Knutsen's Contributions

Any IP created, developed, or contributed by Knutsen specifically at the written direction of the Company in connection with the performance of Services under Exhibit C, and actually delivered to the Company in tangible form, is hereby irrevocably assigned to the Company (or to Michael Leavenworth as Founder prior to formal incorporation). This assignment is worldwide, royalty-free, and covers all patent rights, copyright rights, trade secret rights, and all other intellectual property rights. Knutsen waives any moral rights in such contributions to the fullest extent permitted by law. For the avoidance of doubt, this assignment does not apply to Pre-Existing IP identified in Exhibit A, except to the extent of the license granted in Section 3.1.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, this assignment shall not apply to any IP that Knutsen develops: (i) entirely on his own time; (ii) without use of or reference to the Company's Confidential Information, equipment, or resources; and (iii) without use of or reference to work product created for the Company under this Agreement. Knutsen shall have the burden of demonstrating that any disputed IP meets all three criteria.

> **AMENDMENT §3.2 — Carve-Out Criterion (iii):** Changed "without direct use of work product" to "without use of or reference to." The qualifier "direct" created a gap: Knutsen could argue that reimplementing concepts from Company work product was not "direct use" because he wrote new code from scratch rather than copying. Removing "direct" and adding "reference to" closes this gap — if the independent work could not have been created without reference to Company CI or work product, the carve-out does not apply. This is a one-word change with meaningful impact for the Company's IP protection, and it is consistent with the Residual Knowledge clause in §1.7 which already distinguishes between general know-how (freely usable) and specific CI (restricted).

For the avoidance of doubt, the personal time carve-out in this Section does not override the confidentiality obligations in Section 2, and IP that could not have been created without use of or reference to the Company's Confidential Information does not qualify for the carve-out regardless of whether the three criteria above are otherwise met.

The Parties have identified specific contracted work-for-hire engagements in Exhibit C ("Contracted Work-for-Hire Schedule"). All deliverables produced under Exhibit C are works made for hire to the maximum extent permitted by law and are owned exclusively by the Company. To the extent any such deliverable does not qualify as a work made for hire under applicable law, Knutsen hereby assigns all right, title, and interest in such deliverable to the Company.

### 3.3 Company IP Ownership

The Company retains sole and exclusive ownership of all IP developed by or on behalf of the Company prior to or outside of this Agreement, including but not limited to the Project Green software platform, AI architecture, and all associated documentation, as identified in Exhibit B. Nothing in this Agreement transfers any Company IP to Knutsen.

### 3.4 Further Assurances

Knutsen agrees to execute any documents and take any actions reasonably requested by the Company to perfect, record, or enforce the Company's ownership of assigned IP, at no additional cost to Knutsen. If Knutsen fails to execute such documents within ten (10) business days of written request, Knutsen hereby appoints Leavenworth (or the Company) as attorney-in-fact, coupled with an interest, solely to execute IP assignment and registration documents directly related to New IP created and delivered under Exhibit C of this Agreement. This power of attorney does not extend to Pre-Existing IP listed in Exhibit A, IP developed by Knutsen on his own time under Section 3.2, or any IP developed outside the scope of Exhibit C.

Each deliverable under Exhibit C shall be deemed accepted upon the earlier of: (i) the Company's written acknowledgment of receipt, or (ii) thirty (30) days after delivery without written objection by the Company. The power of attorney in this Section applies to all IP contained in accepted deliverables.

> **AMENDMENT §3.4 — Deliverable Acceptance:** Added acceptance mechanism. The power of attorney applies to IP "created and delivered under Exhibit C," but the original did not define when a deliverable is considered accepted. If Knutsen sent a draft and the Company treated it as final, or if the Company claimed it never received a deliverable Knutsen sent, the POA's applicability would be ambiguous. The 30-day deemed-acceptance provision creates a clear standard: deliver, and if the Company doesn't object within 30 days, it's accepted. Both parties benefit from certainty.

### 3.5 No License to Knutsen

Nothing in this Agreement grants Knutsen any license, right, or interest in any IP owned by the Company or the Founder, except as expressly set forth in the Terms Memo or a separate written agreement signed by both Parties.

### 3.6 Future Licensing

Nothing in this Agreement precludes the Parties from negotiating a separate licensing or revenue-sharing arrangement for such tools in the future. Any such arrangement shall be documented in a separate written agreement signed by both Parties.

### 3.7 Federal IP Protections

The Parties acknowledge that Trade Secrets disclosed under this Agreement are protected under the Defend Trade Secrets Act (18 U.S.C. §1836 et seq.). In the event of willful or malicious misappropriation of Trade Secrets, the Disclosing Party may seek exemplary damages of up to two times (2x) actual damages and attorney's fees as provided under 18 U.S.C. §1836(b)(3) and C.R.S. §7-74-104.

### 3.8 Compensation for Additional IP Contributions

To the extent the Company requests Knutsen to create, contribute, or assign IP beyond the deliverables identified in Exhibit C, any such contribution shall be subject to separate written compensation negotiation prior to Knutsen undertaking such work. No additional IP contribution shall be deemed made under this Agreement without a signed written addendum specifying the scope of work and compensation. The revenue share set forth in the Terms Memo does not constitute compensation for IP contributions beyond Exhibit C.

The Company acknowledges that Knutsen's advisory Services under the Terms Memo are limited to periodic strategic guidance and board participation. Requests for specialized technical work, research, or deliverables beyond this scope shall be subject to this Section 3.8.

> **AMENDMENT §3.8:** Added clarifying sentence about scope of advisory Services. The original §3.8 addresses IP contributions but not advisory time. The Services definition (§1.5) is extremely broad ("all work performed by Knutsen for or on behalf of Project Green... in any other capacity"). Without clarification, the Company could request extensive specialized technical advice in board meetings — advice that doesn't result in tangible IP but requires significant expertise — and argue it falls under "Services" compensated by the 0.25% revenue share. The added sentence makes clear that specialized technical work beyond periodic advisory guidance triggers §3.8's compensation requirement.

### 3.9 Advisor Work Product

Any advice, recommendations, analysis, strategies, or other work product provided by Knutsen in the course of advisory Services under the Terms Memo — as distinct from contracted deliverables under Exhibit C — shall be owned by Knutsen ("Advisor Work Product"). For the avoidance of doubt, Advisor Work Product is not Confidential Information of the Company, and Knutsen may provide similar or identical advice, recommendations, or strategies to other parties, provided that Knutsen does not disclose specific Confidential Information of the Company in doing so. This Section does not apply to deliverables created under Exhibit C, which are governed by Section 3.2.

> **AMENDMENT §3.9 — New Section:** Added Advisor Work Product ownership. Without this clause, the Company could argue that Knutsen's strategic advice — delivered verbally in board meetings — is either (a) Company CI that Knutsen cannot reuse, or (b) IP created "in connection with Services" that is assigned to the Company. Either interpretation would prevent Knutsen from giving similar advice elsewhere, effectively making him exclusive to Project Green. The clause distinguishes between Exhibit C deliverables (assigned to Company) and advisory advice (owned by Knutsen). This is consistent with the General Strategic Advice exclusion in §1.8 and the Residual Knowledge clause in §1.7.

### 4.1 Personal Data

## 6. Remedies

### 6.1 Injunctive Relief

Each Party acknowledges that any actual or threatened breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be an inadequate remedy. Accordingly, the non-breaching Party is entitled to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the requirement to post bond or prove actual damages, in addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity. The Parties acknowledge that a court of competent jurisdiction retains full discretion over the granting of equitable relief.

> **AMENDMENT §6.1:** Added court discretion acknowledgment. The original language could create a false sense of security — contractual provisions purporting to waive bond requirements or stipulate irreparable harm are not always binding on courts. Adding the discretion acknowledgment is honest and does not weaken the clause; it simply reflects legal reality.

### 6.2 Damages

In addition to injunctive relief, the non-breaching Party may recover all actual damages caused by any breach of this Agreement. For the avoidance of doubt, no limitation on damages — including indirect, special, incidental, or consequential damages — shall apply to breaches of the confidentiality or IP provisions in this Agreement. In cases of willful or malicious misappropriation of Trade Secrets, exemplary damages of up to two times (2x) actual damages may be sought under the DTSA and Colorado UTSA.

> **AMENDMENT §6.2:** Added consequential damages preservation. The most significant damages from a CI breach are almost always consequential — lost customers, competitive harm, reputational damage. Without explicit preservation, a future amendment or court interpretation could exclude them. The Association of Corporate Counsel specifically warns against consequential damages disclaimers in NDAs for this reason. Both parties benefit from full damages protection.

### 6.3 Attorney's Fees and Costs

The prevailing Party in any action, suit, or proceeding arising out of or relating to this Agreement, including any appeal, shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorney's fees, court costs, and all other litigation expenses, including but not limited to expert witness fees, e-discovery costs, and fees incurred in establishing entitlement to or the amount of fees and costs.

> **AMENDMENT §6.3:** Expanded attorney's fees clause. The original "reasonable attorney's fees and costs" language has two problems: (1) under Delaware law, generic "costs" may be limited to taxable court costs, excluding expert witnesses and e-discovery; (2) the clause did not specify coverage of appellate proceedings. The revised language enumerates specific cost categories and explicitly covers appeals. Both parties benefit from broader fee recovery.

## 8. General Provisions

### 8.1 Governing Law & Jurisdiction

> **AMENDMENT §8.1:** Strengthened jurisdiction from "consent to" (permissive) to "irrevocably submits to exclusive jurisdiction" (mandatory) with explicit forum non conveniens waiver. The original language could be read as permissive — adding Delaware as an option without excluding other forums. A company in Colorado or Florida could have filed in their own courts despite the Delaware clause. Mandatory exclusive jurisdiction with the waiver eliminates forum shopping by either party.

### 8.1A Mediation

The Parties agree to attempt in good faith to resolve any dispute arising under or relating to this Agreement through mediation before initiating litigation. Mediation shall be conducted by a mutually agreed-upon mediator, with costs shared equally. If the Parties cannot agree on a mediator within fifteen (15) days of a written request for mediation, either Party may proceed to litigation under Section 8.1. Nothing in this Section prevents either Party from seeking injunctive relief under Section 6.1 without first mediating.

> **AMENDMENT §8.1A — New Section:** Added mediation-first requirement. For two individuals in an early-stage startup with a $420 contracted work scope, Delaware litigation is expensive and disproportionate. Mediation is faster, cheaper, and more consistent with the collaborative spirit in Section 7. The injunctive relief carve-out ensures that time-sensitive IP or CI breaches can be addressed immediately in court without waiting for mediation. Both parties benefit from a lower-cost first step before litigation.

### 8.1B Waiver of Jury Trial

EACH PARTY IRREVOCABLY WAIVES THE RIGHT TO TRIAL BY JURY IN ANY ACTION, SUIT, OR PROCEEDING ARISING OUT OF OR RELATING TO THIS AGREEMENT.

> **AMENDMENT §8.1B — New Section:** Added jury waiver. Bench trials (decided by a judge) are faster, cheaper, and decided on law rather than emotion. For a technical IP and confidentiality dispute between two individuals, a judge is better positioned than a jury to evaluate the evidence. Both parties benefit — the less-resourced party (whichever that is at the time of dispute) avoids the cost and unpredictability of jury selection and trial, and the more-resourced party avoids the risk of an emotionally-driven verdict.

> **AMENDMENT §8.6:** Added Delaware UETA citation. The original referenced only the federal E-SIGN Act. Since the Agreement uses Delaware governing law, the directly applicable state statute is Delaware's UETA. Citing both the state and federal statutes provides belt-and-suspenders coverage.

> **AMENDMENT §8.7:** Added deemed-receipt provision. The original said "with written confirmation of receipt" but did not define when notice is effective or what constitutes confirmation. Email delivery is not always reliable, and requiring affirmative acknowledgment gives the receiving party a veto — they could simply never confirm receipt. The deemed-receipt provision establishes a clear default while preserving actual-receipt as the earliest trigger.

Without this clause, if the Company is acquired, the acquirer inherits all rights under this Agreement — including the right to enforce confidentiality against Knutsen for all historical communications and to claim IP rights over future work. An acquirer could weaponize the NDA to restrict Knutsen's activities or demand access to information shared in confidence with Leavenworth personally. Confidentiality obligations survive per §2.6, so the Company's existing CI remains protected — but no new CI flows after termination.

Without a disclaimer, there is an implied representation that shared information is accurate. This protects both parties equally — Leavenworth's early-stage financial projections and Knutsen's security research are both shared "as-is."
