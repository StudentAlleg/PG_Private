## The Two-List Strategy

**List 1 -- Organizations (target firms + whitepaper channels):**
Map every professional accounting organization from AICPA down to local
state societies. Their member directories are the target firm list. Their
publications, conferences, and newsletters are the whitepaper distribution
channels.

**List 2 -- Graduates and new CPAs (influencers):**
Identify recently credentialed accounting professionals (MAcc graduates,
newly licensed CPAs) working inside those target firms. These are
digital-native junior and mid-level staff who are receptive to AI tools
and can champion technology adoption with the managing partners who make
buying decisions.

**How they work together:**

```
Organizations                           Graduates / New CPAs
─────────────                           ────────────────────
Member directories                      University alumni lists
  → target firm list                    State board new-license lists
                                        Beta Alpha Psi chapters
Publications / events                     → influencer list inside
  → whitepaper distribution               those same firms

        ↓                                       ↓
  Top-down awareness                    Bottom-up advocacy
  (partner reads whitepaper)            (staff champion pushes for AI)
        ↓                                       ↓
        └──────────── CONVERGE ─────────────────┘
                          ↓
                Firm evaluates Project Green
```

The whitepaper lands via org channels at firms where young CPAs are
already receptive to AI tools, creating simultaneous top-down and
bottom-up pressure.

## Section 2: Accounting Graduate and New CPA Lists

### University MAcc/MSA/MPA Programs

There are **334 accredited colleges** offering master's degrees in accounting
in the US, producing approximately **15,888 master's degrees per year**.
Undergraduate accounting enrollment reached **267,278 students** in fall 2024
(up 12% from fall 2023).

These programs are the primary pipeline of new accounting professionals
entering firms. Graduates typically start at mid-market and Big Four firms,
making them influencers-in-training at exactly the firms Project Green targets.

#### AACSB Accounting Programs Questionnaire (APQ)

The AACSB maintains the **Accounting Programs Questionnaire** which tracks
enrollment, admissions, degrees conferred, and demographics for all
AACSB-accredited accounting programs. This is the most comprehensive data
source for program sizes. Access is through AACSB directly (aacsb.edu).

### Beta Alpha Psi (Accounting Honor Society)

**Beta Alpha Psi** is the premier honor society for accounting, finance,
and information systems students. It is the single best organized network
of high-performing accounting students and recent graduates.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Active chapters | 300+ at accredited universities |
| Total initiated members (all time) | 370,000+ |
| Chapter accreditation required | AACSB, ACBSP, EPAS, EFMD, or EQUIS |
| Achievement levels | Bronze, Silver, Gold (annual chapter ratings) |
| Website | bap.org |

**Why this matters for lead gen:**
- Beta Alpha Psi members are the top students -- they disproportionately
  land at the best firms and rise to leadership positions
- Chapter directories and events are semi-public
- Gold and Silver chapters are the most active and well-connected
- Many chapters host technology speakers and industry panels --
  a speaking opportunity for Project Green
- Alumni of BAP form strong professional networks that persist into careers

## Section 3: How the Two Lists Work Together

### The Play

1. **Build the firm target list** from organization directories and published
   firm rankings (IPA Top 500, Accounting Today Top 100, state society
   directories, CPAverify).

2. **Build the influencer list** from university MAcc alumni pages, Beta Alpha
   Psi chapter directories, state board new-license data, and LinkedIn searches
   filtered to recent graduates at target firms.

3. **Create whitepapers** on topics relevant to firm partners (AI in accounting,
   CPA shortage solutions, operational efficiency, knowledge management). Use
   research already documented in this project:

   | Whitepaper Topic | Source Material | Target Org Channel |
   |---|---|---|
   | "The AI Readiness Gap in Mid-Market Accounting Firms" | [ai-readiness-assessment.md](ai-readiness-assessment.md), [market-landscape.md](market-landscape.md) | AICPA, state societies, CPA Practice Advisor |
   | "How AI Addresses the CPA Workforce Crisis" | [market-landscape.md](market-landscape.md) (workforce crisis section) | AICPA ENGAGE, Accounting Today, state society newsletters |
   | "From 12 Days to 3: AI and the Month-End Close" | [ai-erp-landscape.md](../research/ai-erp-landscape.md) | IMA, Woodard/Scaling New Heights, AccountingWEB |
   | "Preserving Institutional Knowledge When Partners Retire" | [digital-twins.md](../research/digital-twins.md) | NSA (independent practitioners), state societies, AICPA |
   | "AI for the 10-50 Person Firm: What's Real Today" | [market-gap-analysis.md](market-gap-analysis.md), [product-strategy.md](product-strategy.md) | CPA Practice Advisor, Boomer Consulting circles, Woodard |

4. **Distribute whitepapers** through org channels (contributed articles,
   conference talks, newsletter features, webinar hosting).

5. **Simultaneously target influencers** at those same firms via LinkedIn
   content, Beta Alpha Psi speaking engagements, Young CPA group events,
   and NABA/ALPFA chapter sponsorships.

6. **The two channels converge** when a partner who read the whitepaper asks
   their staff about AI, and the staff member (who's already seen your LinkedIn
   content or attended your BAP talk) says "actually, I've been looking at
   something called Project Green..."
