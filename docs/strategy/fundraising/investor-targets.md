# Investor Target List and Outreach Plan

*A structured pipeline of potential investors for the staged fundraise.
Stage 1 (friends round): $50K-$75K SAFE at $3M-$3.5M cap.
Stage 2 (pre-seed): $300K-$500K SAFE at $6M-$8M cap after revenue.
Organized by tier (highest probability to lowest), with contact strategy
and outreach templates.*

---

## Outreach Strategy Overview

Starting from a cold investor network, the approach must be systematic and persistent.

**Target funnel:**
- 50+ investors identified
- 30-40 contacted (warm intro or cold outreach)
- 10-15 first meetings (Zoom or in-person)
- 5-8 follow-up meetings / due diligence
- 2-5 commitments ($80K-$200K each)
- $300K-$500K closed

**Expected timeline:** 2-4 months from first outreach to close.

**Rule:** Always prefer a warm introduction over cold outreach. Before emailing
any investor directly, check whether anyone in your network (advisors, contacts,
LinkedIn connections) can make an introduction.

## Tier 4: Indirect Channels

People and organizations that can connect you to investors, even if they don't invest themselves.

| Channel | How to Use |
|---------|-----------|
| **Advisory board** | Ask each advisor: "Who do you know that invests in startups?" One warm intro is worth 10 cold emails. |
| **LinkedIn** | Post about building Project Green. Tag South Florida startup community. Like and comment on investor content. Build visibility over 4-6 weeks before pitching. |
| **FAU alumni network** | Mike is an FAU alumnus. University alumni networks often include angel investors and startup founders who can make introductions. |
| **CPA society events** | Not for raising money directly, but for establishing credibility and meeting people who might know investors. |
| **Accounting technology conferences** | AICPA ENGAGE, Intuit Connect, Scaling New Heights. Industry investors attend these events. |
| **Startup meetups** | South Florida has active startup communities. Attend 2-3 events per month. The goal is introductions, not pitches. |
| **AngelList** | Create a profile. Post the raise. Some angels discover deals through the platform. |
| **The Allegiance community** | 20+ years of connections. Some community members may have money or know people who do. Don't overlook non-obvious networks. |

## What Investors Will Ask (Prepare These Answers)

| Question | Prepared Answer |
|----------|----------------|
| "Why should I invest in you vs. a Stanford AI PhD?" | "Because they'd build cool technology and then spend 2 years learning what I already know from 18 years in the industry. Domain expertise is the moat." |
| "Why haven't you quit your job yet?" | "Because I'm disciplined about risk. This capital is what enables full-time commitment. The day the round closes, I give notice." |
| "Owen is part-time and just graduated. Where's your technical team?" | "Owen goes to expanded role with this funding, and the first engineering hire is in the use of funds. The advisory board -- including a senior cybersecurity analyst and an AI engineer -- provides interim technical leadership." |
| "You have zero revenue. Why should I believe in $40K ACV?" | "Because 6 independent benchmarks validate it. Firms pay $42K-$60K for managed IT. They pay $35K-$53K for Karbon with zero AI. And the ROI math on one FTE's recovered capacity pays for the product in year one." |
| "What if Intuit just builds this into QuickBooks?" | "They will add AI features. But they're structurally constrained to QBO data only. They will never integrate with Xero, Gusto, or a firm's internal documents. We're the multi-system intelligence layer Intuit can't be." |
| "What's your unfair advantage?" | "I'm not building for accountants. I am one. I've reviewed the work of 60 accountants. I know which problems are real, which solutions firms will actually adopt, and which features are academic." |
| "How do I get my money back?" | "Three paths: (1) We grow to $10M+ ARR and go public or get acquired. At 10x revenue, that's a $100M+ outcome. (2) A strategic acquirer (Intuit, Thomson Reuters, Microsoft) buys the data moat and the customer base. (3) We're profitable and can distribute dividends if we choose a slower path. But our plan is the venture path -- build a category-defining company." |
