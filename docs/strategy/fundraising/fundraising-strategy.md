## Why Raise Now

The decision to pursue external capital is driven by opportunity cost, not survival:

1. **Competitors are moving.** 60+ funded startups, $1.2B+ raised in AI accounting. The adoption window (2025-2028) is narrowing. Every quarter without go-to-market execution is ground ceded to funded competitors.

2. **The opportunity exceeds bootstrap capacity.** A two-person team working evenings and weekends cannot capture a $228M SAM. The product works. The market research is done. What's missing is execution speed.

3. **Alpha momentum.** A real customer said yes. Converting that verbal commitment into a deployed product -- and using that deployment to close 3-5 more firms -- requires full-time focus and resources.

4. **The AI timing window is open but closing.** AI adoption in accounting jumped from 9% to 41% in one year (2024-2025). By 2028, incumbents will have competitive AI features. First movers with data moats will win.

## Capital Efficiency

The founder's personal and business economics make this raise unusually capital-efficient:

**Founder personal position:**
- Monthly personal expenses: ~$4,000/month ($48K/year)
- Existing income from ML Financial Group (accounting practice since 2017): $30K/year
- W-2 salary (current employer): covers the remaining gap while employed
- Personal safety net: ~$60K ($40K 401K + $20K investments) — 6-month personal emergency fund, separate from the business

**Business operating costs (Project Green, today — all self-funded by founder):**
- Owen (co-founder, part-time): $115/week at 5 hrs/week (~$460/month); authorized to double to 10 hrs/week (~$920/month)
- AI tools (Cursor, models, inference): ~$200/month (variable — can scale up or down)
- Subscriptions, domains, office tools: ~$50-$100/month
- **Current business burn: ~$710-$760/month (~$9K/year)**
- **With Owen doubled to 10 hrs: ~$1,170-$1,220/month (~$14K/year)**

The founder is self-funding the entire business today — including co-founder compensation — out of pocket. The product is built. The evaluation framework runs. The alpha customer is secured. **No external capital is required for the business to continue operating.** The raise is pure growth capital — every dollar goes to legal foundation and scaling, not survival.

This is the strongest possible position for a pre-seed raise: a founder who doesn't need the money, a product that already works, a co-founder already being paid, and a business that costs ~$1,200/month to run. Investors fund acceleration, not desperation.

## Staged Approach

The fundraise is structured in three stages, each gated by milestones. The first stage is intentionally small -- it is designed to be closeable with one or two checks, proves the model with revenue, and sets up a much stronger position for the second raise.

| **Target raise** | $50K-$75K |
| **Valuation cap** | $3M-$3.5M (post-money) |
| **Implied dilution at conversion** | ~1.4-2.5% |
| **Investor profile** | Strategic investor (Parr3 LLC -- current employer, prospective first deployment customer), possibly 1-2 angels |

**Why Parr3 as lead investor:** Parr3 is a full-service accounting firm with 60 accountants and 90 HNW clients -- exactly the type of firm Project Green serves. If they invest and deploy, "our first investor is an accounting firm that runs on the product" is the strongest possible social proof for every subsequent conversation. A $50K-$75K check is a size a firm owner can authorize without board approval or extensive diligence.

**Use of funds:**

|----------|--------|---------|
| Legal & entity formation | $15K | Attorney review + opinion, C-Corp formation, IP assignment, E&O insurance |

Note: the founder's personal expenses ($4K/month) are covered by the W-2 salary and ML Financial Group income ($30K/year). The founder already self-funds the business at ~$750-$1,200/month (Owen at $460-$920/month, AI tools ~$200/month, subscriptions ~$75/month). If Parr3 invests at the higher end of the range ($75K), the additional $25K extends the reserve and enables a third conference or early engineering contractor work.

**Milestone this capital achieves:** Entity formed, legally clear, alpha deployed, 1-3 paying customers, first revenue. The business already runs on ~$750-$1,200/month (self-funded by the founder); this capital funds the legal foundation and the growth activities that the founder cannot self-fund.

### Why the Three-Stage Approach Preserves Equity

Raising $2M all at once at a $4M pre-money valuation means giving up ~33% of the company. By proving the model in stages -- first with a small friends round, then pre-seed with revenue, then seed with strong ARR -- each subsequent round happens at a higher valuation. The same ~$2M total raised results in ~17% total investor dilution instead of ~33%. The staged approach saves Mike roughly 16 percentage points of ownership.

The friends round is the key unlock: $50K at $3.5M cap is only ~1.43% dilution and is closeable with one check. The founder takes no salary and already self-funds the entire business (including co-founder compensation) at ~$1,200/month -- there is no survival component to this raise. Compare to raising $400K at $4.5M cap (~8.9% dilution) with no revenue and no entity. The friends round gets you to revenue for a fraction of the cost and dilution, and every dollar after that is raised from a position of strength.

## Key Risks to Address Before Pitching

### 1. Employment Agreement

Mike's confidentiality agreement with Parr3 LLC (current employer) includes a $100K per-breach liquidated damages clause, IP assignment provisions, and non-solicitation terms. The agreement is governed by California law, which provides strong protections for employee-created inventions under California Labor Code Section 2870 (inventions developed on own time, own equipment, not relating to employer's business) and California Business & Professions Code Section 16600 (non-competes void). The agreement itself incorporates the Section 2870 carve-out.

**Status:** Attorney briefing document prepared (see `Legal Documents/attorney-briefing.md`). Attorney review must be completed before any investor conversation. The facts strongly favor Mike under California law, but a written legal opinion is required for investor diligence.

**For the Parr3 pitch specifically:** If Parr3 invests, the employment agreement issue is resolved by mutual agreement rather than adversarial interpretation. This is one of the strategic advantages of Parr3 as the first investor.

### 2. Pre-Revenue Positioning

Frame as "pre-revenue with a working product" -- not "no traction." The prototype has 120+ automated tests, a structured evaluation framework, and runs on real accounting data structures. We're iterating toward production accuracy. This is not a slide deck and a promise.

### 3. Part-Time Co-Founder

Owen is currently part-time (5 hrs/week). VCs will ask about team commitment. Counter: "Stage 2 capital brings Owen to 20+ hours/week, and we hire senior engineering talent. Owen has been with the project since inception and has a 20+ year collaborative relationship with the founding team."

### 4. Alpha Is the Founder's Own Client

The alpha customer is Mike's personal accounting client of 9 years. VCs may question independence. Counter: "We're dogfooding -- the founder is the first customer. And the alpha is a healthcare practice, not an accounting firm, which validates cross-vertical potential before we've even started selling to our primary ICP."

### 5. No Investor Network

Starting from cold on investor relationships means a longer fundraising cycle (3-6 months vs. 4-8 weeks for a well-connected founder). Mitigation: Stage 1 bypasses this entirely by targeting a known relationship (Parr3). Stage 2 benefits from having Parr3 as a reference investor. Systematic outreach through angel networks, advisory board introductions, and conference visibility for Stage 2+. See [investor-targets.md](investor-targets.md) for the full target list.
