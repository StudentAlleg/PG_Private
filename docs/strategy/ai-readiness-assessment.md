# AI Readiness Assessment for Accounting Firms

> A structured framework for evaluating an accounting firm's readiness
> to benefit from AI. Designed as a lead-generation tool: deliver genuine
> value to the firm owner while creating a natural conversation path
> toward Project Green.
>
> **Status**: Draft -- refine after first 3 conversations with firm owners
>
> **Date**: February 2026

---

## Purpose

The AI Readiness Assessment serves three goals simultaneously:

1. **Value to the firm owner**: They get an honest, structured view of where their firm stands relative to AI adoption, with specific recommendations they can act on regardless of whether they work with us.

2. **Positioning for Mike**: Conducting the assessment positions Mike as a knowledgeable advisor on AI in accounting, not a salesperson pushing a product.

3. **Pipeline for Project Green**: The assessment naturally surfaces whether a firm is a good fit for the product. Firms that score well are candidates for a demo. Firms that score poorly get genuine advice on how to improve. Both outcomes build trust and reputation.

---

## How to Use This

### Before You Have Conversations

- Read through the assessment dimensions to internalize them
- You don't need to use the formal framework in every conversation
- The dimensions are a mental model for understanding firm readiness

### During an Exploratory Conversation

- Weave assessment questions naturally into conversation
- "How does your team typically find answers to procedural questions?"
- "What accounting software are you using? How long have you been on it?"
- "Have you tried any AI tools in the firm?"
- Take notes against the dimensions

### As a Formal Offering

- "We've developed an AI readiness assessment specifically for accounting firms. It takes about 30 minutes and you'll get a one-page summary of where your firm stands and what the highest-value AI opportunities are. No cost, no obligation."
- Walk through the dimensions in order
- Score as you go (or immediately after)
- Deliver the one-page summary within 48 hours

## Assessment Dimensions

### Dimension 1: Data Readiness (0-20 points)

How accessible, structured, and clean is the firm's data?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **0-5** | Desktop-only software (e.g., QuickBooks Desktop, lacerte desktop). No cloud access to data. Manual Excel-based processes for reporting. |
| **6-10** | Cloud accounting software (QBO, Xero) but data is messy -- uncategorized transactions, inconsistent naming, backlogs. Limited use of the software's features. |
| **11-15** | Cloud software used well. Most transactions categorized. Chart of accounts is organized. Reports are generated from the system, not manually in Excel. |
| **16-20** | Clean, well-organized cloud data. Consistent naming conventions. Regular reconciliation. The firm could hand their data to a new accountant and they'd understand it quickly. |

**Questions to ask**:
- "What accounting software do you use? Desktop or cloud?"
- "If I looked at your chart of accounts, would I understand it in 5 minutes?"
- "How many uncategorized transactions do you typically have at any given time?"
- "Where does your firm's data actually live? All in one system or spread across several?"

### Dimension 2: Process Documentation (0-20 points)

How much of the firm's operational knowledge is written down?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **0-5** | Nothing is documented. Processes live in people's heads. When someone leaves, their knowledge leaves with them. |
| **6-10** | Some documentation exists (a few SOPs, an employee handbook, some checklists) but it's outdated, incomplete, or not used consistently. |
| **11-15** | Key processes are documented and reasonably current. New hires have something to reference. Not everything is written down, but the important things are. |
| **16-20** | Comprehensive documentation. SOPs for major workflows. Regularly updated. Staff actually use the documentation rather than just asking a senior. |

**Questions to ask**:
- "If a new hire started tomorrow, what would you hand them to get up to speed?"
- "Are your month-end close procedures written down somewhere?"
- "When was the last time your SOPs were updated?"
- "What happens when a senior accountant goes on vacation? How does the team handle their work?"

### Dimension 3: Technology Stack (0-20 points)

How modern and integrated is the firm's technology?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **0-5** | Mostly desktop software. Limited cloud adoption. Paper files still common. Staff resistant to technology changes. |
| **6-10** | Some cloud tools but not integrated. QuickBooks cloud but email-based document sharing. Multiple disconnected systems. |
| **11-15** | Cloud-first stack. Accounting, payroll, and document management are cloud-based. Some integrations between systems. Staff comfortable with technology. |
| **16-20** | Integrated cloud stack. Systems talk to each other (QBO + Gusto + document management). APIs or integrations actively used. Firm embraces technology as a competitive advantage. |

**Questions to ask**:
- "Walk me through the tools your team uses on a typical day."
- "How do your systems talk to each other? Or do they?"
- "What's your biggest technology frustration right now?"
- "Has the firm adopted any new technology in the last 12 months?"

### Dimension 4: Team Capacity and Culture (0-20 points)

Does the firm have the capacity and willingness to adopt AI?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **0-5** | Severely capacity-constrained. Staff are working overtime just to keep up. No bandwidth for anything new. Culture is "we've always done it this way." |
| **6-10** | Busy but functional. Some interest in improvement. One or two champions but leadership hasn't prioritized technology. |
| **11-15** | Leadership is actively looking for ways to improve efficiency. There's at least one person who would champion a new tool. Firm has adopted new tools before and it went reasonably well. |
| **16-20** | Innovation-friendly culture. Leadership explicitly prioritizes technology adoption. Staff are encouraged to find better ways of working. The firm has budget for new tools. |

**Questions to ask**:
- "How stretched is your team right now? On a scale of 1-10?"
- "Is there someone at the firm who gets excited about new technology?"
- "What's the last new tool the firm adopted? How did that go?"
- "How does your firm handle the CPA shortage? Hiring? Outsourcing? Overtime?"

### Dimension 5: AI Awareness (0-20 points)

How much does the firm know about AI and what's realistic?

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **0-5** | No awareness or active skepticism. "AI is hype." Have not tried ChatGPT or any AI tool. |
| **6-10** | Aware that AI exists in accounting. May have tried ChatGPT personally but haven't applied it to firm operations. Curious but uncertain. |
| **11-15** | Have experimented with AI tools (ChatGPT for drafting, Copilot in Excel, basic automation). Understand that AI could help but don't know where to start. |
| **16-20** | Actively evaluating AI solutions. Have researched specific tools. May have attended webinars or conferences about AI in accounting. Ready to invest if the right solution exists. |

**Questions to ask**:
- "Has anyone at the firm used ChatGPT or similar tools for work?"
- "What's your general feeling about AI in accounting? Excited? Skeptical? Somewhere in between?"
- "Have you evaluated any AI-specific accounting tools?"
- "If AI could solve one problem for your firm, what would it be?"

## Scoring and Interpretation

### Score Calculation

Simply add the five dimension scores. Maximum is 100.

### Score Bands

| Score Range | AI Readiness Level | What It Means |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **0-30** | Early Stage | The firm has fundamental prerequisites to address before AI makes sense. Focus on cloud migration, data cleanup, and basic process documentation first. AI investment now would likely fail. |
| **31-50** | Foundation Building | The pieces are coming together but gaps remain. The firm would benefit from targeted improvements (data quality, documentation, one or two cloud integrations) that would make AI adoption successful in 6-12 months. |
| **51-70** | Ready to Start | The firm has the foundation for AI adoption. Data is reasonably accessible, some processes are documented, and there's willingness to try. This is the ideal time to pilot an AI tool -- the firm is ready but hasn't yet committed to a solution. |
| **71-85** | Strong Position | Well-positioned for AI. Clean data, documented processes, modern tech stack, and an innovation-friendly culture. The question isn't whether AI will work here, but which specific applications deliver the most value. |
| **86-100** | Leading Edge | Ahead of most firms. Likely already using some AI tools. The opportunity is in deeper, more integrated AI solutions (multi-agent systems, process optimization, client-facing AI) rather than basic copilot features. |

### Dimension-Level Insights

Beyond the total score, the individual dimension scores tell a story:

| Pattern | Interpretation | Recommendation |
|---------|---------------|----------------|
| High data, low documentation | "The data is there but the knowledge is locked in people's heads" | AI-powered knowledge capture could be transformational (Archetype 1 fit) |
| High technology, low AI awareness | "Modern stack but haven't connected the AI dots" | Education and a demo can convert quickly -- they have the infrastructure |
| High culture, low data | "Willing but not ready" | Help them get data-ready first; they'll be a great customer in 6-12 months |
| Low across the board | "Not ready for AI" | Be honest. Recommend foundational steps. Build trust by not overselling. |
| High across the board | "Why haven't they adopted AI yet?" | Likely a timing or awareness issue. A demo could close fast. |

## Follow-Up Playbook

### After Delivering the Assessment

**For firms scoring 51+** (Ready to Start or above):

> "Based on your assessment, your firm is well-positioned to benefit
> from AI. I've been building a prototype specifically for accounting
> firms -- an AI assistant that can answer questions about financial
> data and firm procedures. Would you be interested in seeing a quick
> demo? It takes about 10 minutes."

If they say yes, proceed to the [Demo Playbook](demo-playbook.md).

**For firms scoring 31-50** (Foundation Building):

> "Your firm has solid fundamentals. The two areas I'd focus on before
> investing in AI are [specific gap 1] and [specific gap 2]. Happy to
> share some resources on how other firms have tackled those. And when
> you're ready to explore AI, I'd love to continue the conversation."

This builds trust and creates a future pipeline. Follow up in 3-6 months.

**For firms scoring 0-30** (Early Stage):

> "Honestly, AI isn't the right investment for your firm right now.
> The highest-value thing you could do is [specific foundational step].
> I'm happy to point you toward some resources."

Radical honesty builds more trust than a sales pitch. These firms will
remember that you didn't waste their time. Some will come back when
they're ready.

## Iteration Plan

This assessment framework is a starting point. It should evolve based
on real conversations:

- **After 3 assessments**: Review which questions generated the best
  insights. Drop questions that don't produce useful information. Add
  questions that came up naturally in conversation.

- **After 5 assessments**: Look for patterns. Are certain dimensions
  consistently higher or lower than expected? Does the scoring correlate
  with interest in the product? Adjust weights if needed.

- **After 10 assessments**: Consider publishing aggregated, anonymized
  findings as content. "We assessed 10 accounting firms' AI readiness.
  Here's what we found." This is powerful thought leadership.
