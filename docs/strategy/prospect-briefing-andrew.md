## 1. What We're Selling

We build a firm's **AI twin** -- a private, firm-specific assistant that connects to the accounting firm's existing software (general ledger, payroll, tax, document storage) and lets staff ask questions in plain English. Instead of interrupting a partner or digging through five different systems, anyone at the firm can ask a question and get an accurate, cited answer drawn from the firm's own data and documents.

**The tagline: "Ask this before you ask a human."**

It's not a replacement for any software they already use. It sits on top of everything and makes it all queryable. Think of it as giving the firm a digital version of its most knowledgeable partner -- one that's available 24/7, never forgets a procedure, and never gets annoyed at being asked the same question twice.

**What this looks like in practice:**

- A junior associate asks: "What's our policy on capitalizing expenses over $500?" -- the AI answers using the firm's own written procedures.
- A manager asks: "What was Client X's AR balance last month?" -- the AI pulls it from the general ledger and gives a cited answer.
- A new hire asks: "How do we handle month-end close for clients on cash basis?" -- the AI walks them through the firm's documented process.

The product starts as an **internal staff tool** (no client-facing risk). It reduces partner interruptions, speeds up onboarding for new hires, and ensures everyone gets consistent answers. Over time, it expands to client-facing explanation and process modeling -- but the entry point is internal-only.

## 2. Ideal Client Profile

We are not looking for "accounting firms" broadly. We are looking for this specific type of firm:

### Primary Target

| Attribute | What to Look For | Why |
|-----------|-----------------|-----|
| **Firm size** | 10-50 employees (more is okay) | Large enough to feel the pain, small enough to decide fast |
| **Services** | Tax + bookkeeping / business management | Both functions use the same data; maximum value from one integration |
| **General ledger** | QuickBooks Online is ideal, but any cloud GL works | Our first integration; most common in this segment |
| **Payroll** | Gusto (preferred) or ADP | Second integration priority |
| **Pain point** | Capacity constraints -- too much work, not enough people | The CPA shortage is our entry wedge |
| **Decision maker** | Managing partner or ops partner who feels the pain personally | Not a committee decision; one person who can say yes |
| **Technology stance** | Open to AI but hasn't committed to a solution yet | Easier to win a new category than displace an incumbent |

### Bigger Firms (Secondary Target, Later)

| Attribute | What to Look For |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Firm size** | 50-200 employees |
| **Ownership** | PE-backed (the operating partner is the champion) |
| **Services** | Full-service (tax, audit, advisory, business management) |
| **Pain point** | Operational standardization across acquired practices |

### Walk Away From

These firms are not a fit right now:

- **Solo practitioners** -- too small, can't justify the cost or the engagement
- **Desktop-only software** -- no cloud API to integrate with; we need cloud-based systems
- **No documented processes at all** -- part of what we do is make their knowledge queryable; if there's nothing written down, there's nothing to work with yet
- **Active M&A transition** -- too distracted to adopt new technology

## 4. The Offer

We are looking for firms willing to be **pilot partners**. The arrangement:

### What They Get (Free)

- AI assistant connected to their general ledger (QuickBooks or other cloud GL)
- Their SOPs, policies, and procedures loaded into the knowledge base
- 3-5 of their staff using it daily
- Weekly check-ins and rapid iteration based on their feedback
- Direct access to the founding team for support

### What We Get

- Real data flowing through the system (not synthetic test data)
- Real users telling us what works and what doesn't
- Permission to use anonymized results as a case study
- A path to a paid engagement once the pilot proves value

### How to Frame It

This is a **mutual investment**. They invest their time and data; we invest our product and support. The pilot runs for approximately 90 days. If it works, we convert to a paid annual contract. If it doesn't, they walk away having lost nothing but time.

### For Context: What This Is Worth

When we convert pilots to paid customers, pricing looks like:

| Component | Price |
|-----------|-------|
| **Setup** | $5,000-$15,000 one-time (integration, configuration, knowledge loading) |
| **Monthly platform** | $2,000-$5,000/month (depends on firm size) |
| **Ongoing tuning** (optional) | $1,000-$3,000/month (monthly check-ins, new use cases, knowledge updates) |

**Annual contract value: $30,000-$70,000 per firm.** This is comparable to what firms pay for managed IT services or a fractional CFO -- a budget category they already understand.

Pilot partners get in at no cost. That's the hook: they're getting $30K-$70K/year of value for free in exchange for being early and giving us feedback.

### Success Criteria

How we know the pilot is working:

- The system answers questions without engineering intervention
- Financial queries return accurate, verifiable answers (>90% accuracy)
- At least 3-5 staff members use it regularly within the first 30 days
- Feedback is captured and issues are addressed within 24 hours
- By end of pilot, the firm sees enough value to discuss a paid arrangement

## 7. How to Approach

### The Door-Opener: Free Assessment

The lowest-friction way in: *"Let us look at your firm's systems and processes for 30 minutes. We'll show you where AI can help -- no commitment, no cost."*

This works because:
- It's free (no risk for them)
- It's short (30 minutes, not a day-long engagement)
- It delivers value immediately (they learn something about their own firm)
- It naturally leads to the pilot conversation

### What to Say

Focus on the **pain**, not the technology:

| Say This | Because |
|----------|---------|
| "The CPA shortage is hitting firms like yours hard. We help you do more with the team you have." | Speaks to the capacity constraint they feel every day |
| "Your partners are spending hours answering questions their team should already know the answer to." | Directly names the Managing Partner's pain |
| "Every time a senior person leaves, institutional knowledge walks out the door with them." | The knowledge retention problem is real and emotional |
| "New hires at your firm could get answers on day one instead of waiting months to learn the ropes." | Concrete outcome, easy to visualize |
| "We connect to the systems you already use -- QuickBooks, your payroll, your documents -- and make them all queryable." | No rip-and-replace; works with what they have |

### What NOT to Say

| Avoid This | Because |
|------------|---------|
| "AI will replace your staff" | Creates fear and resistance; we are augmenting, not replacing |
| Technical jargon (agents, orchestration, vector databases, language models) | They don't care how it works; they care what it does |
| "We're like an ERP" | Triggers comparison to massive, expensive systems we're not competing with |
| Overpromising on timeline or capabilities | Be honest about what works today and what's coming; trust is everything |

### The Pitch Flow

1. **Open with the pain:** "I work with accounting firms that are stretched thin -- too much work, not enough people, partners spending half their day answering questions."
2. **Introduce the concept:** "We build an AI assistant that's trained on your firm's own data and documents. Your team asks it questions in plain English and gets accurate, cited answers."
3. **Make it concrete:** "Imagine a new hire asking 'how do we handle month-end close for cash-basis clients?' and getting an answer from your firm's own procedures -- instantly, without bothering a senior."
4. **Offer the assessment:** "We'd love to take 30 minutes to look at your systems and show you where this could help. No cost, no commitment."
5. **If interest:** Move to pilot conversation. Explain the free 90-day arrangement.
