### TAM / SAM / SOM

| Metric | Calculation | Value |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| **TAM** (Total Addressable Market) | All US accounting firms that could use AI operational intelligence | ~$8B/year (80,000 firms x $100K avg. annual AI spend potential) |

These are directional estimates. The key insight: even 50 firms at a modest price point creates a meaningful early-stage business, and the market is deep enough to support $100M+ ARR at scale.

## Market Dynamics -- Five Forces Shaping the Landscape

### 1. PE Consolidation Wave

Private equity is rewriting the ownership structure of accounting:

- **One-third of the top 30 US firms** may be PE-backed by 2026
- Succession crisis drives deals: nearly three-quarters of CPAs are retirement-age
- PE attraction: stable, recurring, "annuity-like" revenue
- PE playbook: acquire, centralize operations, standardize delivery, invest in technology, exit at higher multiple
- **Over 80,000 firms** remain fragmented -- massive runway for continued consolidation

**What PE-backed firms need (and will pay for):**
- Operational clarity (how does work actually flow?)
- Standardized processes across acquired firms
- Margin optimization data
- M&A readiness metrics
- Technology-driven efficiency gains to justify premium multiples at exit

**Project Green implication:** The Process Twin (Archetype 3) is the PE product. PE operating partners are a channel, not just a customer segment.

### 2. CPA Workforce Crisis

- ~75% of CPAs reaching retirement age
- Firms cannot replace departing expertise through hiring alone
- Junior staff onboarding is slow (tribal knowledge, undocumented processes)
- Partner time is the most expensive resource; it's being consumed by repetitive questions

**Project Green implication:** Archetype 1 (Staff Copilot) directly addresses this -- capturing institutional knowledge in an AI that junior staff can query instead of interrupting seniors.

### 3. AI Adoption Inflection

- Adoption: 9% (2024) to 41% (2025) -- a 4.5x jump in one year
- 77% of firms plan to increase AI investment
- 35% already use AI tools daily
- 82% of early adopters see positive ROI within first year
- Month-end close: 12 days to 3 days with AI automation
- Gartner: by 2028, one-third of enterprise software includes agentic AI

**Project Green implication:** The window is open. Firms are actively looking for AI solutions. But the window will narrow as incumbents add AI features.

### 4. Big Four Investment

Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, and EY have committed a combined **$9.5 billion** to AI transformation:

- Building proprietary tools for their own practices
- Offering digital twin consulting services to large clients
- Not productizing for the SMB/mid-market
- Creating awareness and demand that smaller firms can't satisfy internally

**Project Green implication:** The Big Four validate the market and create demand. They are not competitors at our market segment -- they serve firms 100x larger than our target. They are, indirectly, our best marketing.

### 5. Rising Client Expectations

- Clients increasingly expect real-time financial visibility
- "Why did my profit drop?" should not require a phone call and a 3-day wait
- Advisory services (fractional CFO, strategic planning) are growing faster than compliance work
- Firms that can't deliver proactive insights will lose clients to those that can

**Project Green implication:** Archetype 2 (Explanation Engine) meets this demand. Archetype 1 enables the firm to be more responsive even without client-facing AI.

## Competitive Moat Analysis

What makes Project Green defensible over time:

### 1. Firm-Specific Data Accumulation

Every day the product runs, it accumulates more data about how that specific firm operates. The AI gets better at answering that firm's questions, understanding their terminology, and modeling their processes. This creates **natural switching costs** -- migrating to a competitor means losing the accumulated intelligence.

### 2. Process Knowledge Network Effects

As we onboard more firms, we (with appropriate anonymization) can identify patterns:
- "Firms like yours typically close in X days"
- "Top-performing firms in your segment do Y differently"
- Benchmarking data that no single firm can generate alone

Each new firm makes the benchmarking more valuable for every existing firm. This is a **cross-firm network effect.**

### 3. Integration Depth

Deep integrations with QuickBooks, Xero, Gusto, ADP, ProConnect, and firm file servers are expensive to build and maintain. Each integration is a barrier to entry for new competitors who would have to build the same connectivity from scratch.

### 4. Layer 2 Services Relationship

The professional services wrapper creates a **human relationship** on top of the software. Firms trust the people who set up and tune their AI twin. This relationship-based switching cost compounds with the data-based switching cost.

### 5. PE Operating Partner Channel

If we establish ourselves as the AI platform that PE operating partners recommend to their portfolio firms, we create a **distribution channel that scales with PE activity.** PE firms acquire new accounting practices regularly; each acquisition is a new Project Green deployment.

## The Timing Window

The market window is open but will not stay open indefinitely:

- **2025-2026:** Adoption inflection. Firms are actively evaluating AI. Early movers have advantage.
- **2027-2028:** Market consolidation. Winners emerge. Incumbents (Intuit, Microsoft) ship more capable AI features.
- **2029+:** Mature market. Late entrants face established competitors with deep data moats.

We must have a working product with paying customers by end of 2026 and be scaling by 2027 to capture the window.
