## Domain Availability Research: Original 14 Candidates

Domain availability was checked via web search, WHOIS lookups, and company existence research. Statuses are classified as:

- **TAKEN** — Domain is registered and actively used by an existing company
- **PARKED/REGISTERED** — Domain appears registered but no active business found (likely squatted or held)
- **LIKELY AVAILABLE** — No evidence of registration or active use found in any search
- **CONFLICT** — Existing company in adjacent space creates trademark or brand confusion risk

## Revised Top 3 Recommendations

### Recommendation #1: Provna

**Why this is now the strongest candidate:**

- Completely clean namespace — zero search results for this name anywhere
- Both .com and .ai are likely available
- "Provenance" is the perfect root concept: the product establishes the provenance of firm intelligence — where every data point came from, how every answer was derived
- Five letters, two syllables (PROV-nah) — short, clean, memorable
- The concept of provenance carries weight in accounting (audit trail), finance (chain of custody), and legal (evidence chain) — all three target verticals
- Passes the managing partner test without hesitation
- Works at every scale: "Provna" on a seed deck, "Provna" on an S-1
- "The Provna platform" / "Powered by Provna" both work naturally
- No technology trend baked into the name — it ages well
- Distinctive enough to own search results from day one

**Ticker potential:** PRVN

---

### Recommendation #2: Acuvis

**Why this is the second choice:**

- Completely clean namespace — no competing entities found
- Both .com and .ai are likely available
- Latin roots (acuere + vis) = "sharpened sight" — directly communicates the value proposition
- Three syllables, strong phonetics, immediately pronounceable
- Classical roots signal seriousness and permanence to conservative buyers
- The "acu-" prefix evokes accuracy, acuity, and acumen — all on-brand
- Works internationally (Latin roots parse globally)
- "Acuvis Intelligence" or just "Acuvis" both work as entity names

**Risk:** Acuvis Treuhand AG exists in Switzerland — a fiduciary/accounting firm in Canton Uri (founded 2020, CHF 100K capital, operates at acuvis.ch). This is an accounting-adjacent company, which creates international trademark risk that must be assessed by counsel. Also phonetically adjacent to ACUVI (small IT company) and AcuView (energy monitoring software). None are direct US competitors but the Swiss entity is a real flag.

**Ticker potential:** ACVS

---

### Recommendation #3: Depthmark

**Why this is the third choice:**

- Completely clean namespace — no competing entities found
- Both .com and .ai are likely available
- Nautical metaphor is distinctive and memorable — a depth mark shows what's below the surface
- Two everyday English words combined into something original
- Evokes measurement, precision, and hidden depth — all on-brand
- Works at scale: "Depthmark" on a conference badge, "Depthmark" on an S-1
- No pronunciation ambiguity, no spelling confusion
- Conservative managing partners understand "depth" as a value concept

**Risk:** Two-word compound names can feel less institutional than single-word names at IPO scale. "Depthmark" reads more like a startup name than "Provna" or "Acuvis." This is a style preference, not a dealbreaker.

## Key Takeaway

Domain research eliminated 10 of the original 14 candidates. The Round 1 top recommendation (Clearfold) is compromised — the .com is owned by a door company. Of the 29 total candidates evaluated, only 7 have both clean domain landscapes AND no meaningful company conflicts.

The board recommends **Provna** as the new top candidate. It is the only name in the full set that combines: zero namespace conflicts, both domains likely available, a root concept (provenance) that maps directly to all three target verticals, and the institutional gravitas needed from seed deck to S-1 filing.
