**Prepared by:** Richard (Board Chair, Project Green)

**Purpose:** Fresh candidate slate with pre-screened legal/domain clearance for Delaware C-Corp formation

---

## Methodology

This round applies a different discipline than Rounds 1 and 2. Every candidate was screened BEFORE inclusion against six dimensions:

1. **Web search "{name}.com"** --- must show no active company or registered domain
2. **Web search "{name}.ai"** --- must show no active company or registered domain
3. **Web search "{name} company"** --- no existing companies in tech, finance, accounting, or professional services
4. **Web search "USPTO {name} trademark"** --- no active trademarks in software/technology/financial services classes
5. **Web search "{name} Delaware corporation"** --- no registered Delaware entities found
6. **Web search "SEC EDGAR {name}"** --- no public company filings found

Over 80 candidate names were generated, searched, and evaluated. More than 65 were eliminated during pre-screening. Only the names below survived all six checks.

**Zero names from Rounds 1 or 2 are repeated.** This is an entirely fresh slate.

## Top 3 Recommendations

### Recommendation #1: Censight

**Why this is the strongest candidate:**

- The most immediately comprehensible name in the entire set --- no explanation required
- "Censight" communicates the exact value proposition: seeing with authority and precision
- One compound word, two syllables in natural speech --- the shortest and most memorable name presented
- Passes the managing partner test without hesitation: it sounds like something that already exists on enterprise contracts
- Works at every scale: "Censight" on a seed deck, "Censight" on an S-1, "Censight" on a conference lanyard
- "The Censight platform" / "Powered by Censight" both work naturally
- Zero namespace conflicts --- this name is a true blank canvas
- Does not lock the company into any technology trend, vertical, or metaphor
- The "cen-" prefix carries institutional weight (census, centurion, center) without requiring explanation
- Clean across all six pre-screening dimensions

**Risk:** The only risk is that "Censight" could be perceived as a compound of "cen" + "sight" or misread as "censored sight." In practice, the natural reading is overwhelmingly positive. Brand guidelines should establish the pronunciation (SEN-site) from day one.

**Ticker potential:** CSGT

---

### Recommendation #2: Verathos

**Why this is the second choice:**

- The strongest classical gravitas in the entire set --- "veritas" + "ethos" in a single name
- Three syllables with a commanding cadence: ver-AH-thos
- The name tells a story without explaining it: truth and character, fused
- Conservative buyers (accounting firm managing partners) respect names with classical weight
- Works internationally --- Latin and Greek roots parse across all Western languages
- IPO-ready: "Verathos, Inc." on a stock exchange listing would carry immediate credibility
- Zero namespace conflicts across all dimensions
- The "-thos" ending is distinctive but not obscure --- it echoes "ethos," a word every executive knows

**Risk:** Requires establishing pronunciation in early sales conversations. This is manageable and arguably a feature: the name invites a 10-second brand story that builds trust.

**Ticker potential:** VRTH

---

### Recommendation #3: Certivus

**Why this is the third choice:**

- Latin "certus" (certain/sure) is the root of "certify," "certain," "certificate" --- all words that resonate deeply with accounting and professional services buyers
- The "-ivus" suffix mirrors Acuvis (which the Chairman liked) but with a root meaning that maps directly to the product: certainty
- Three syllables, clean phonetics: SER-tih-vus
- "Certivus" suggests a system that makes things certain --- the exact promise of a business intelligence platform
- Works naturally in enterprise contexts: "Certified by Certivus" / "The Certivus platform"
- Zero namespace conflicts across all dimensions
- The name carries built-in trust signals for the target buyer

**Risk:** Phonetic proximity to "Certiv" (a Seattle AI agent security startup, launched March 2026 with $4.2M). The names are clearly different (Certivus vs. Certiv) and the markets are different (enterprise decision intelligence vs. endpoint AI security), but this should be flagged for trademark counsel given the timing.

**Ticker potential:** CTVS

## Screening Methodology Disclaimer

Web search can identify known conflicts but cannot definitively confirm availability. The statuses above reflect what is discoverable through public web search as of March 17, 2026. The following direct database queries are required before entity formation:

1. **Domain registration:** Attempt actual registration of .com and .ai at a registrar (the only definitive test)
2. **USPTO TESS:** Direct search of the Trademark Electronic Search System (web search cannot access the full database)
3. **Delaware Division of Corporations:** Direct entity name search at icis.corp.delaware.gov
4. **Florida Sunbiz:** Direct search at search.sunbiz.org
5. **SEC EDGAR:** Direct company search at sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar

The board recommends running all five direct queries for the Chairman's top 2-3 finalists immediately.
