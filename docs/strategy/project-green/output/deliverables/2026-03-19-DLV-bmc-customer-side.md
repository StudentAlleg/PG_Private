## 1. Customer Segments

### Anchor Segment: Mid-Tier Firms ($5B Revenue Target)

Malik's direction is clear: the anchor target is firms generating around $5B in revenue. These are the large, established firms -- many PE-backed or PE-targeted -- that have the budget, the operational complexity, and the strategic urgency to adopt AI-powered operational intelligence. They are not "small firms experimenting with AI." They are firms where operational standardization, knowledge retention, and margin optimization are board-level priorities.

But Project Green does not stop at the anchor. The architecture serves the full spectrum -- from $2M firms drowning in the CPA shortage to $5B firms preparing for PE exits. The segmentation below reflects that range, anchored at the top.

### Segment Map

| Segment | Firm Revenue | Employees | Why They Buy | Entry Timing |
|---------|-------------|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Anchor: Large / PE-Backed** | $100M-$5B+ | 500-50,000+ | Operational standardization across acquired practices. Exit readiness. Margin optimization. Process Twin is the product. | Phase 3-4 (2027-2028+) -- but relationship-building starts now |
| **Growth: Mid-Market** | $5M-$100M | 50-500 | Cross-practice coordination, multiple data systems, staffing complexity. Full-service needs. Higher willingness to pay. | Phase 2-3 (2027+) |
| **Beachhead: Small Firms** | $200K-$5M | 10-50 | CPA shortage pain, knowledge loss from retiring partners, capacity constraints. The proving ground. | Phase 1 (2026) -- alpha customer already secured |
| **Adjacent: Outsourced/Virtual Firms** | Varies | 5-30 | Cloud-native by design. Serve many clients (more data per firm). Margin pressure drives efficiency adoption. | Phase 1-2 opportunistically |

### Sub-Segments Within Each Tier

**Within the Anchor ($100M-$5B+):**
- PE-backed roll-ups needing operational clarity across 5-20 acquired practices
- Top 100 firms (Accounting Today list) investing in AI but building nothing firm-specific
- Full-service firms (tax, audit, advisory, business management) with the most complex workflows

**Within Mid-Market ($5M-$100M):**
- Multi-office firms struggling with process consistency across locations
- Firms with dedicated ops/tech partners who champion technology adoption
- Firms transitioning from compliance to advisory services (higher margins, more data needs)

**Within the Beachhead ($200K-$5M):**
- Tax + bookkeeping firms (primary ICP) -- both functions use the same data, maximum value from one integration
- Industry-specialized firms (healthcare, real estate, nonprofit) -- deep domain compliance needs
- The "almost ready" segment: 45% of firms using AI ad hoc with no strategy, actively looking for guidance

### Who Is Involved in the Purchase Decision

This varies dramatically by firm size, and the BMC needs to account for all tiers:

**At $5B anchor firms:**
- **Financial Decision Maker:** Chief Operating Officer or PE Operating Partner. This is a portfolio-level decision, not a practice-level one.
- **End Users:** Hundreds to thousands of staff across acquired practices. They don't choose -- they get deployed to.
- **Influencers:** Practice managing partners at individual offices who report results up. IT/innovation leadership who evaluate technical fit.
- **Champion:** The PE operating partner who needs standardization metrics for exit readiness.

**At mid-market firms ($5M-$100M):**
- **Financial Decision Maker:** Managing partner or executive committee. More likely a committee decision at this size.
- **End Users:** Senior accountants, managers, associates across multiple service lines.
- **Influencers:** Ops partner (if one exists), tech-forward senior staff, outside consultants (Boomer Consulting circles, Woodard Group members).
- **Champion:** The ops partner who feels the pain of inconsistent processes and knowledge loss personally.

**At beachhead firms ($200K-$5M):**
- **Financial Decision Maker:** Managing partner -- one person who can say yes. Not a committee.
- **End Users:** Junior associates, senior accountants, bookkeepers.
- **Influencers:** Recently credentialed staff (MAcc graduates, new CPAs) who are digital-native and push for AI adoption from below.
- **Champion:** The managing partner who spends half their day answering questions their team should already know.

### What "Job" Are They Hiring Project Green to Do

The job-to-be-done shifts by segment, but the thread is consistent:

| Segment | Primary Job to Be Done |
|---------|----------------------|
| **$5B anchor** | "Give me operational visibility across all my portfolio firms so I can standardize, optimize margins, and prove exit readiness." |
| **Mid-market** | "Help me coordinate across service lines, retain institutional knowledge, and transition from compliance to advisory." |
| **Beachhead** | "Stop my partner from being the bottleneck. Let my team get answers without interrupting the most expensive person in the building." |
| **Outsourced/virtual** | "Help me serve more clients with the same team. Every efficiency gain drops straight to margin." |

## 2. Customer Relationships

### How Do Customers Find Out About Similar Products/Services?

Accounting firms are conservative buyers. They do not browse Product Hunt. They learn about new tools through a specific, trust-based ecosystem:

**1. Peer networks (highest influence)**
- Conversations with other managing partners at CPA society events, Boomer Consulting peer circles, Woodard Group community
- "What are you using?" is the most powerful question in accounting firm technology adoption
- The IPA Top 500 and Accounting Today Top 100 firms set the tone -- smaller firms follow what the leaders adopt

**2. Industry publications and conferences**
- *Journal of Accountancy* (AICPA flagship, 597K member reach)
- *CPA Practice Advisor* (41K print circulation, 11 digital issues/year)
- *Accounting Today* (major publication, publishes the Top 100 list)
- AICPA ENGAGE (thousands of attendees, 42 CPE credits)
- Scaling New Heights (Woodard, Jun 14-17 2026, Orlando -- bookkeeping/CAS focused)
- State CPA society annual meetings (where managing partners actually show up)

**3. Technology consultants and advisors**
- Boomer Consulting (300+ top firm leaders in peer circles)
- Accounting technology consultants who build tech stack recommendations
- QBO ProAdvisors (accountants who recommend tools to other accountants)
- Right Networks (cloud hosting platform used by thousands of firms)

**4. PE operating partners (for anchor segment)**
- PE firms that have acquired accounting practices hire operational consultants
- They benchmark against Deloitte's digital twin consulting ($300K-$500K/engagement)
- They talk to each other through PE operating partner networks

**5. Bottom-up advocacy from staff**
- Recently credentialed CPAs and MAcc graduates (15,888 master's degrees per year) are digital-native
- Beta Alpha Psi honor society (370,000+ initiated members) represents high-performing graduates
- Young CPA committees at state societies are exactly the influencer profile
- These staff members push technology adoption upward to managing partners

### How Will Project Green Create Awareness?

The strategy is already well-defined in the source docs. It maps to a dual-positioning framework:

**Lead Generation Mode (opens doors):**
"We help accounting firms navigate the AI transformation." Problem-first, not product-first. Positions the founders as experts and advisors, not vendors. Works before the product is "complete." The AI Readiness Assessment is the bridge tool -- a free, consultative offer that creates a conversation.

**Product Mode (converts interest):**
"Ask this before you ask a human." Outcome-based, speaks directly to the partner's pain. Demonstrated through live prototype with 94.4% accuracy.

**Specific awareness channels:**

| **LinkedIn content engine** | 1-3 posts/week on CPA shortage, AI in accounting, firm operations | Compounding organic reach | Phase 1 (ongoing) |
| **Whitepapers via org channels** | "The AI Readiness Gap in Mid-Market Firms" distributed through AICPA, state societies, CPA Practice Advisor | 41K+ print; 597K AICPA member base | Phase 1-2 |
| **Conference speaking** | State CPA society events, Scaling New Heights, AICPA ENGAGE | Hundreds to thousands per event | Phase 2 |

| **PE operating partner network** | Direct relationships with PE firms that own accounting practices | 1 PE firm = 5-20 portfolio deployments | Phase 3+ |

### How Will Project Green Get and Keep Customers?

**Getting customers (acquisition):**

The sales motion is phased and deliberate:

1. **Phase 1 -- Founder-Led (first 5 firms):** Personal relationships. Warm introductions to managing partners. Free assessment offer. 30-day pilot with 3-5 staff. Convert to annual contract. Alpha customer already secured (2/22/2026) at 99% discount -- testing PMF, not price.

2. **Phase 2 -- Referral-Driven (firms 6-20):** Every happy firm generates referrals to 2-3 peers. Case studies fuel thought leadership. QBO ProAdvisor network as a channel.

3. **Phase 3 -- PE Channel (20+ firms):** PE operating partners as a distribution channel. One relationship = multiple firm deployments. Industry association partnerships.

**Keeping customers (retention):**

Project Green has structural retention built into the architecture:

- **Firm-specific data accumulation:** Every day the product runs, it gets better at that firm's questions, terminology, and processes. Switching means losing accumulated intelligence. Switching costs exceed $500K for mid-market vertical SaaS customers.
- **Cross-firm network effects:** As the portfolio grows, anonymized benchmarking becomes more valuable for every firm. "Firms like yours close in 5 days. You close in 9. Here's what top performers do differently."
- **Integration depth:** Deep connections to QBO, Xero, Gusto, ADP, document stores. Each integration is expensive to replicate.
- **Services relationship (Layer 2):** The professional services wrapper -- setup, tuning, monthly check-ins -- creates a human relationship on top of the software. Firms trust the people, not just the product.
- **Expansion revenue:** Firms start with Archetype 1 (Copilot), expand to Archetype 2 (Explanation Engine), then Archetype 3 (Process Twin). NRR above 120% expected.

## 3. Channels

### How Do Customers Want to Be Reached?

Accounting firms are not impulse buyers. They move through a trust-based evaluation process that looks like this:

1. **Awareness:** They hear about a category (AI for accounting) through peers, publications, or conferences -- not through ads or cold emails.
2. **Consideration:** They evaluate through personal conversations, demos from someone they trust, and references from firms they respect.
3. **Decision:** A managing partner (small firm) or committee/PE operating partner (large firm) makes the call. They want to see it work on data that looks like theirs.
4. **Onboarding:** They expect hand-holding. Small firms don't self-onboard when the product touches their financial data. They want a human who understands their business.

### Where Do They Buy Similar Products Today?

Firms currently acquire technology through these channels:

| Channel | What They Buy There | Relevance to Project Green |
|---------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| **Direct vendor sales** | Practice management software (Karbon, Canopy), GL systems (QBO, Xero) | Direct sales is the primary channel. Project Green follows this model with founder-led sales scaling to a sales team. |
| **Technology consultants** | Tech stack recommendations, implementation services | Consultants are a referral channel. Accounting technology consultants (Boomer, Woodard ecosystem) recommend tools to firms. |
| **QBO ProAdvisor network** | Accounting software, add-ons, integrations | ProAdvisors recommend tools to each other and to clients. A warm channel once the QBO integration is live. |
| **PE operating partners** | Operational tools for portfolio firms | PE partners standardize technology across their portfolio. One decision = many deployments. This is the anchor channel for $5B firms. |
| **Industry conferences** | Discovery, not direct purchase -- but the handshake happens here | AICPA ENGAGE, Scaling New Heights, state CPA society events are where managing partners meet vendors they later buy from. |
| **Peer referral** | Everything -- the most trusted channel in professional services | The flywheel depends on this. One firm, one case study, warm intros to peer firms. |

### The Two-List Convergence Play

The lead-gen strategy has a specific mechanism worth noting for the canvas: two lists that converge on the same firms from different directions.

**List 1 -- Organizations (top-down):** Member directories from AICPA, state CPA societies, IPA Top 500, Accounting Today Top 100. These give us firm names and managing partner contacts. Whitepapers distributed through their publications create awareness at the partner level.

**List 2 -- Influencers (bottom-up):** Recent MAcc graduates, newly licensed CPAs, Beta Alpha Psi alumni working inside those same firms. Reached through LinkedIn, university career reports, Young CPA groups, NABA/ALPFA chapter events.

When a partner reads a whitepaper about AI in accounting at the same time their junior staff is pushing for AI tools they saw on LinkedIn -- that convergence is the buying trigger.
