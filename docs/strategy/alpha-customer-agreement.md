# Alpha Test Customer Agreement

## Milestone

**Date:** February 22, 2026

Project Green has secured its first alpha test customer. This is the first external validation of the product and the beginning of our transition from prototype to production delivery.

## Agreement Terms

| Term | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Discount** | 99% off standard pricing for the duration of the alpha period |
| **Duration** | Unspecified -- runs until both parties agree the alpha phase is complete |
| **Our obligation** | Deliver a production-level system the customer can use in their daily operations |
| **Their obligation** | Serve as our alpha test partner: provide real-world feedback, surface bugs, validate use cases |

The discount reflects the reality that this is a mutual investment. The customer accepts the risk of using an early product; we accept the revenue trade-off to gain irreplaceable real-world validation.

## Strategic Significance

### What This Changes

1. **Urgency.** We now have a commitment to deliver. Prototyping speed-of-learning shifts to production speed-of-delivery. Every sprint must move the product closer to something a real firm can rely on.

2. **Quality bar.** "Works in demo" is no longer sufficient. The system must handle real data, recover from errors gracefully, and earn trust through reliability. Security, error handling, and resilience are now requirements, not nice-to-haves.

3. **Feedback loop.** We gain access to the single most valuable input for a startup: a real customer using the product in their real workflow, telling us what matters and what doesn't.

4. **Market credibility.** A working alpha deployment -- even at 99% discount -- is proof that the product exists and that a firm trusted us enough to use it. This is the foundation of every future sales conversation.

### What This Does NOT Change

- **The ICP.** One customer does not redefine the target market. We validate assumptions against this customer's profile, but the broader ICP stands until we have data suggesting otherwise.

- **The product vision.** We are still building an Internal Staff Copilot (Archetype 1). The alpha engagement is not an invitation to build custom features that pull us off the roadmap.

- **The phased approach.** We still sequence through Phase 1 milestones. The alpha customer is the forcing function that makes Phase 1 real, not a reason to skip steps.

## Success Criteria for the Alpha Engagement

| Criteria | Measurement |
|----------|-------------|
| **System is operational** | Customer can query their firm's data through the chat interface without engineering intervention |
| **Answers are trustworthy** | Financial queries return verifiable, cited answers with >90% accuracy |
| **Reliability** | System uptime sufficient for daily use; errors are handled gracefully, not silently |
| **Customer adoption** | At least 3-5 staff members use the system regularly within the first 30 days |
| **Feedback captured** | Structured feedback mechanism in place; issues logged and triaged within 24 hours |
| **Path to paid conversion** | By end of alpha, customer sees enough value to discuss transition to a paid arrangement |

## What We Owe the Customer

- A working system connected to their real data (QuickBooks Online integration)
- Firm-specific knowledge base (their SOPs, policies, procedures via RAG)
- Responsive support during the alpha period (bugs addressed within 24 hours)
- Regular check-ins to capture feedback and demonstrate improvements
- Transparency about what the system can and cannot do

## What the Customer Provides Us

- Access to their QuickBooks Online data (with appropriate authorization)
- Documents for the knowledge base (SOPs, policies, handbooks)
- Staff availability for testing and feedback sessions
- Honest assessment of what works and what doesn't
- Permission to use anonymized outcomes as a case study (negotiated separately)

## Risk Acknowledgment

This engagement carries specific risks documented in the [Risk Register](risk-register.md):

- **Product readiness gap.** The current prototype scores 3/10 on production readiness. Significant hardening is required before the alpha customer can use it.
- **Reputation risk.** A poor alpha experience damages our credibility in a small, networked market.
- **Scope creep.** The customer's specific needs may pull us away from the product roadmap.
- **Resource strain.** Supporting a live customer while continuing development stretches a small team.

These risks are accepted deliberately. The value of real-world validation outweighs the cost of managing these risks, provided we manage them actively.
